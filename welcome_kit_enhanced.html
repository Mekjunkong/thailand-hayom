Pro Tip: Download Grab and Bolt apps BEFORE you land. They work better than regular taxis and show upfront prices.
✈️

First 24 Hours Checklist

  • Get Thai SIM card at airport (see Module 3)
  • Exchange some cash (5,000-10,000 THB recommended)
  • Download essential apps: Grab, Bolt, Google Translate, Google Maps
  • Save hotel address in Thai language for taxi drivers
  • Set up mobile banking/payment apps
  • Register with Israeli Embassy (optional but recommended)

Visa Information

Israeli Citizens:
• Visa on Arrival: 30 days free (if arriving by air)
• Extension: Can extend 30 more days at immigration office (1,900 THB)
• Documents needed: Passport valid 6+ months, return ticket, hotel booking
• Important: Overstaying costs 500 THB per day (max 20,000 THB)

2. Top Scams to Avoid

IMPORTANT: Thailand is generally safe, but tourists are common targets for scams. Stay alert!

Common Scams

1. "Temple is Closed" Scam

How it works: A friendly local tells you the temple/palace is closed for a Buddhist holiday or cleaning. They offer to take you to a "better" temple or gem shop instead.

Reality: Major temples are rarely closed. This leads to overpriced gem shops or tour packages.

How to avoid: Ignore strangers near tourist sites. Check temple hours online. Walk to the entrance yourself.

⚠️

2. Tuk-Tuk "10 Baht Tour"

How it works: Driver offers incredibly cheap city tour (10-20 THB). Takes you to gem shops, tailor shops where they get commission.

Reality: You waste 2-3 hours shopping, face high-pressure sales tactics.

How to avoid: Use Grab/Bolt instead. If using tuk-tuk, agree on price and destination first. Say "NO SHOPPING" clearly.

3. Jet Ski Rental Damage Scam

How it works: After returning jet ski, operator claims you damaged it. Demands 30,000-50,000 THB cash immediately.

Reality: Damage was pre-existing or fabricated.

How to avoid: Take photos/video of jet ski BEFORE renting. Rent from reputable shops with good reviews. Never pay cash for "damages" - call tourist police 1155. Consider skipping jet skis entirely - parasailing is safer.

Emergency Contact: Tourist Police 1155 (English speaking, 24/7)
