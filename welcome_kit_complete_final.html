2. ⚠️ Top Scams to Avoid

🚨 IMPORTANT: Thailand is generally safe, but tourists are common targets for scams. Stay alert!

Common Scams

1. "Temple is Closed" Scam

How it works: A friendly local tells you the temple/palace is closed for a Buddhist holiday or cleaning. They offer to take you to a "better" temple or gem shop instead.

Reality: Major temples are rarely closed. This leads to overpriced gem shops or tour packages.

How to avoid: Ignore strangers near tourist sites. Check temple hours online. Walk to the entrance yourself.

2. Tuk-Tuk "10 Baht Tour"

How it works: Driver offers incredibly cheap city tour (10-20 THB). Takes you to gem shops, tailor shops where they get commission.

Reality: You waste 2-3 hours shopping, face high-pressure sales tactics.

How to avoid: Use Grab/Bolt instead. If using tuk-tuk, agree on price and destination first. Say "NO SHOPPING" clearly.

3. Jet Ski Rental Damage Scam

How it works: After returning jet ski, operator claims you damaged it. Demands 30,000-50,000 THB cash immediately.

Reality: Damage was pre-existing or fabricated.

How to avoid: Take photos/video of jet ski BEFORE renting. Rent from reputable shops with good reviews. Never pay cash for "damages" - call tourist police 1155. Consider skipping jet skis entirely - parasailing is safer.

4. Ping Pong Show Overcharge

How it works: "Free entry" to ping pong show. Bill arrives: 5,000-10,000 THB for drinks. Bouncers block exit.

Reality: Drinks are marked up 50x normal price.

How to avoid: Ask for menu with prices BEFORE ordering. Walk out if no menu. Call tourist police if threatened.

5. Taxi Meter "Broken"

How it works: Taxi driver says meter is broken, quotes flat rate (usually 3-5x normal price).

Reality: Meter works fine, driver wants to overcharge.

How to avoid: Use Grab/Bolt. If taxi, insist on meter. If refused, take another taxi.

💡 Emergency Contact: Tourist Police 1155 (English speaking, 24/7)

4. 🚕 Transportation Rules & Tips

Ride-Hailing Apps (BEST OPTION)

Grab (Most Popular)

  • Works like Uber - upfront pricing, cashless payment
  • Available in all major cities
  • Options: GrabCar (regular), GrabCar Premium, GrabBike (motorcycle taxi)
  • Average cost: 50-150 THB for most city trips

Bolt (Cheaper Alternative)

  • Usually 10-20% cheaper than Grab
  • Fewer drivers, longer wait times
  • Same features as Grab

InDriver (Negotiable Prices)

  • You set your price, drivers counter-offer
  • Can get very cheap rides
  • Requires more effort/negotiation

Taxis

⚠️ Warning: Many Bangkok taxis refuse to use meter or refuse short trips. Grab/Bolt is more reliable.

If using taxi:

  • Always insist on meter ("meter please" or "meet-ter")
  • Starting fare: 35 THB
  • Typical 5km trip: 60-100 THB
  • You pay tolls (20-70 THB) on top of meter
  • Tip not expected but appreciated (round up to nearest 10)

Renting Motorbike/Scooter

🚨 CRITICAL INFORMATION:
• You MUST have International Driving Permit (IDP) to legally rent/ride
• Police checkpoints are common - fine is 500-1,000 THB without IDP
• Accidents without IDP = insurance won't cover you
• Helmet is MANDATORY (200 THB fine if caught without)

Rental Tips:

  • Price: 150-300 THB/day depending on bike and location
  • Take photos of ALL damage before riding away
  • Check brakes, lights, horn before accepting bike
  • Never leave passport as deposit - photocopy only
  • Get insurance if offered (50-100 THB/day, worth it!)

ATM Withdrawals

⚠️ High Fees: Thai ATMs charge 220 THB per withdrawal + your bank's foreign transaction fee (usually 2-3%)

How to minimize ATM fees:

  • Withdraw maximum amount per transaction (usually 20,000-30,000 THB limit)
  • Use ATMs inside banks (safer, better maintained)
  • Best banks for tourists: Bangkok Bank, Kasikorn Bank (purple), SCB
  • Always choose "WITHOUT CONVERSION" when asked (let your home bank convert)

How Much Cash to Carry Daily

  • Budget traveler: 1,500-2,500 THB/day
  • Mid-range: 3,000-5,000 THB/day
  • Luxury: 8,000+ THB/day

8. 🌙 Nightlife Safety Guide

Drink Safety

🚨 CRITICAL: Drink spiking happens. Follow these rules:
• Never leave drink unattended
• Watch bartender make your drink
• Don't accept drinks from strangers
• Stick with friends, use buddy system
• If you feel unusually drunk, tell friends immediately

  • Keep emergency cash (200-500 THB) separate from wallet

9. 🛕 Temple Etiquette

Dress Code (STRICTLY ENFORCED)

⚠️ You WILL be denied entry if not dressed properly:
• Shoulders MUST be covered (no tank tops, sleeveless shirts)
• Knees MUST be covered (no shorts, short skirts)
• No see-through clothing
• No ripped jeans with holes
• Remove shoes before entering temple buildings

What to wear:

  • Long pants or long skirt
  • T-shirt or shirt with sleeves
  • Closed shoes or sandals (easy to remove)
  • Some temples rent sarongs/shawls (20-50 THB deposit)

Behavior Rules

  • Remove shoes: Before entering any temple building
  • Feet position: Never point feet at Buddha images. Sit with feet tucked to side
  • Head respect: Don't touch Buddha statues or monk's heads
  • Women & monks: Women cannot touch monks or hand items directly (place on cloth/table)
  • Photography: Usually allowed but ask first. No selfies with Buddha in disrespectful poses
  • Silence: Speak quietly, turn phone to silent
  • Donations: Optional but appreciated (20-100 THB)

10. 🛍️ Shopping & Bargaining

Where to Bargain vs Fixed Price

BARGAINING EXPECTED:
• Street markets (Chatuchak, night markets)
• Floating markets
• Beach vendors
• Tuk-tuk rides
• Tailor shops

FIXED PRICE (don't bargain):
• Malls and department stores
• 7-Eleven, Family Mart
• Restaurants
• Grab/Bolt rides
• Pharmacies

How to Bargain Politely

  1. Ask the price: "Tao-rai krap/ka?" (How much?)
  2. Show interest but don't commit: Look at item, check quality
  3. Offer 40-50% of asking price: Smile and counter-offer
  4. Negotiate up slowly: Meet somewhere in middle (usually 60-70% of original)
  5. Walk away technique: If price too high, walk away. They often call you back with better price
  6. Buy multiple items: "If I buy 3, what price?" gets better deals
  7. Stay friendly: Smile, joke, don't get angry. It's a game!
💡 Example:
Vendor: "This shirt 500 baht"
You: "Too expensive! 200 baht?" (smile)
Vendor: "No no, 450"
You: "250 baht, final price" (start walking)
Vendor: "OK OK, 300 baht for you!"
You: "Deal!" (shake hands)

11. 🏖️ Beach Safety & Water Activities

Swimming Safety

⚠️ DANGER - Read This Before Swimming:
• Red flags = DO NOT SWIM (strong currents, jellyfish)
• Rip currents are common - if caught, swim parallel to shore
• Jellyfish season (Oct-Jan) can be dangerous
• Some beaches have strong undertow
• Alcohol + swimming = deadly combination

Water Activities Prices

Activity Price Range Safety Tips

Jet Ski (30 min) 1,000-1,500 THB AVOID - common scam target (see Module 2)

Best Beaches by Region

  • Kata Beach: Good for families, cleaner, surfing

  • Ao Nang: Main beach, good base for island hopping

Koh Samui

  • Chaweng Beach: Most developed, nightlife
  • Lamai Beach: Quieter than Chaweng, good swimming

13. 📍 3-Day Bangkok Itinerary

Day 1: Historic Bangkok

Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

  • 8:00 AM: Breakfast at hotel or local café
  • 9:00 AM: Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew (allow 2-3 hours)
  • Cost: 500 THB entry + 100 THB for audio guide
  • Tip: Arrive early to beat crowds and heat

Afternoon (12:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

  • 12:00 PM: Lunch at Tha Tien Market (street food, 60-100 THB)
  • 1:00 PM: Wat Pho (Reclining Buddha) - 200 THB
  • 2:30 PM: Cross river to Wat Arun (ferry 5 THB, temple entry 100 THB)
  • 4:00 PM: Relax at hotel or explore Khao San Road

Evening (6:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

  • 6:00 PM: Sunset at Wat Arun or rooftop bar
  • 7:30 PM: Dinner cruise on Chao Phraya River (800-1,500 THB) OR street food at Yaowarat (Chinatown)
  • 9:00 PM: Night market shopping or massage (250-400 THB/hour)

Day 2: Modern Bangkok & Shopping

Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

  • 9:00 AM: BTS to Chatuchak Weekend Market (if Saturday/Sunday) OR MBK Center (weekday)
  • Cost: Shopping budget 1,000-3,000 THB
  • Tip: Bring cash, wear comfortable shoes, stay hydrated

Afternoon (12:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

  • 12:00 PM: Lunch at mall food court (80-150 THB)
  • 2:00 PM: Visit Jim Thompson House Museum (200 THB) OR Erawan Shrine
  • 4:00 PM: Shopping at Siam Paragon, CentralWorld, or Platinum Fashion Mall

Evening (6:00 PM - 11:00 PM)

  • 6:00 PM: Rooftop bar for sunset (Vertigo, Sky Bar, Octave) - drinks 300-600 THB
  • 8:00 PM: Dinner at Thonglor/Ekkamai (trendy area) - 400-800 THB
  • 10:00 PM: Nightlife at RCA or Sukhumvit Soi 11

Day 3: Markets & Culture

Morning (6:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

  • 6:00 AM: Early start for Damnoen Saduak Floating Market (1.5 hours from Bangkok)
  • Option: Book tour (600-1,200 THB) OR DIY with minivan + longtail boat
  • Alternative: Maeklong Railway Market (train passes through market!)

Afternoon (12:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

  • 12:00 PM: Return to Bangkok, lunch at Asiatique
  • 2:00 PM: Thai massage at Wat Pho school (300-500 THB) OR spa treatment
  • 4:00 PM: Last-minute shopping or visit Lumpini Park

Evening (6:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

  • 6:00 PM: Sunset at Wat Saket (Golden Mount) - 50 THB
  • 7:30 PM: Farewell dinner at riverside restaurant
  • 9:00 PM: Night market at Asiatique or Rod Fai Market

Total Estimated Cost (3 Days)

  • Budget: 4,500-7,000 THB (accommodation not included)
  • Mid-Range: 8,000-12,000 THB
  • Luxury: 15,000-30,000+ THB
