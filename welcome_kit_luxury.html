Grab/Bolt 250-400 THB 45-90 min Door-to-door convenience

💡 Pro Tip

Download Grab and Bolt apps BEFORE you land. They work better than regular taxis and show upfront prices. Make sure you have mobile data immediately upon arrival!

✈️

First 24 Hours Checklist

  • Get Thai SIM card at airport (see Module 3 for detailed comparison)
  • Exchange some cash - recommended 5,000-10,000 THB for immediate expenses
  • Download essential apps: Grab, Bolt, Google Translate, Google Maps
  • Save hotel address in Thai language for taxi drivers
  • Set up mobile banking and payment apps
  • Register with Israeli Embassy (optional but recommended for safety)

Visa Information for Israeli Citizens

📋 Visa Requirements
  • Visa on Arrival: 30 days free (if arriving by air)
  • Extension: Can extend 30 more days at immigration office (1,900 THB)
  • Documents needed: Passport valid 6+ months, return ticket, hotel booking
  • Important: Overstaying costs 500 THB per day (max 20,000 THB)

Top Scams to Avoid

⚠️ IMPORTANT

Thailand is generally safe, but tourists are common targets for scams. Stay alert and informed to protect yourself!

Common Tourist Scams

1. "Temple is Closed" Scam

How it works: A friendly local approaches you near major temples (Grand Palace, Wat Pho) and tells you the temple is closed for a Buddhist holiday or special ceremony. They offer to take you to a "better" temple or gem shop instead.

Reality: Major temples are rarely closed during tourist hours. This is a setup to take you to overpriced gem shops or tour packages where the scammer gets commission.

How to avoid: Ignore strangers near tourist sites offering unsolicited advice. Check temple hours online beforehand. Walk to the entrance yourself to verify if it's really closed.

⚠️

2. Tuk-Tuk "10 Baht Tour"

How it works: Tuk-tuk driver offers an incredibly cheap city tour (10-20 THB) to see temples and attractions. Sounds like a great deal! However, they take you to gem shops, tailor shops, and massage parlors where they earn commission.

Reality: You waste 2-3 hours being pressured to buy overpriced items. The "tour" is just a shopping trip disguised as sightseeing.

How to avoid: Use Grab or Bolt instead of tuk-tuks. If using a tuk-tuk, agree on price and destination first. Say "NO SHOPPING" clearly before getting in.

3. Jet Ski Rental Damage Scam

How it works: You rent a jet ski at the beach. After returning it, the operator claims you damaged it and demands 30,000-50,000 THB cash immediately. They may threaten to call police or become aggressive.

Reality: The damage was pre-existing or completely fabricated. This is a well-known scam in Pattaya and Phuket.

How to avoid: Take photos and video of the jet ski from all angles BEFORE renting. Rent only from reputable shops with good online reviews. Never pay cash for "damages" - call tourist police 1155 instead. Consider skipping jet skis entirely - parasailing is safer and more fun!

🚨 Emergency Contact

Tourist Police: 1155 (English speaking, 24/7 service)

SIM Card Guide

Top 3 Providers Comparison

Provider Tourist Package Price Best For
AIS 15 days unlimited data + 100 THB calls 299 THB Best coverage in islands and rural areas
DTAC 15 days unlimited data 249 THB Best value for money in cities
True 30 days unlimited data + free WiFi hotspots 399 THB Longest validity, good for extended stays
💡 Pro Tip

Get your SIM card immediately at the airport - don't wait until you reach the city! You'll need Google Maps and Grab right away to navigate. All providers have booths in the arrival hall.

Where to Buy

  • Airport: Most convenient, available 24/7 at arrival halls (Suvarnabhumi, Don Mueang, Phuket, Chiang Mai airports)
  • 7-Eleven: Available everywhere, but may require Thai language skills
  • Provider stores: AIS, DTAC, True stores in shopping malls for better customer service
📱 What You Need
  • Unlocked phone (check with your carrier before traveling)
  • Passport for registration (required by Thai law)
  • Cash (most airport booths accept credit cards too)
