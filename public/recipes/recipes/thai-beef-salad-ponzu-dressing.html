Thai Beef Salad with Ponzu Dressing — A Japanese-Thai Fusion Delight

⏱ 23 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

This innovative Thai beef salad bridges two culinary worlds, combining the zesty brightness of traditional Thai yam with the subtle complexity of Japanese ponzu sauce. The result is a refreshing dish that maintains authentic Thai flavors while adding an intriguing umami twist that will surprise and delight your taste buds.

thai saladbeef recipesfusion cuisinequick dinnerhealthy meals
💡 This unique dish represents the beautiful fusion of Thai and Japanese flavors that's become popular in modern Thai cuisine. The ponzu sauce adds a subtle umami depth while maintaining the bright, tangy profile that Thai salads are famous for.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or ribeye, sliced thin
  • 2 tablespoons ponzu sauce
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 cups mixed fresh vegetables (cucumber, tomatoes, onions), chilled
  • 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat a non-stick pan or grill pan over medium-high heat without oil. Grill the beef slices for 2-3 minutes per side until just cooked through but still tender.
  2. While the beef is still warm, slice it into thin strips against the grain for maximum tenderness.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together ponzu sauce, light soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, chili powder, and lime juice to create the dressing.
  4. Add the warm beef strips to the dressing and toss gently to coat evenly.
  5. Arrange the chilled fresh vegetables on a serving plate and top with the dressed beef.
  6. Garnish with thinly sliced garlic and fresh cilantro before serving immediately.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Choose beef with slight marbling for the most tender results — avoid lean cuts that can become tough
  • Never overcook the beef as it will continue cooking slightly from residual heat when mixed with the dressing
  • Serve immediately while the beef is still warm and the vegetables are cold for the perfect temperature contrast
