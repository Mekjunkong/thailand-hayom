Thai Rice Porridge with Ground Pork — Comforting Khao Tom Moo Sab

⏱ 30 min 🍽 Serves 3-4 📊 Easy

Nothing beats a steaming bowl of khao tom moo sab when you need pure comfort in a bowl. This Thai rice porridge with ground pork is the kind of soul-warming dish that Thai families turn to for breakfast, late-night cravings, or whenever someone needs a little culinary hug.

thai foodrice porridgecomfort foodeasy dinnerground pork
💡 Khao tom is Thailand's ultimate comfort food, often enjoyed as a late-night snack or gentle breakfast after a long night out. Street vendors serve this nourishing porridge 24/7, and every Thai person has their preferred vendor with just the right consistency and seasoning.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old)
  • 5.3 oz ground pork
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4-5 cups pork stock or water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper, or to taste
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot or wok over medium-high heat. Add a splash of oil and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant and golden, about 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add the ground pork to the pot and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until no longer pink and slightly browned, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Pour in the pork stock or water and bring to a boil. Add the cooked rice and stir gently to break up any clumps.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice breaks down and creates a porridge-like consistency.
  5. Season with fish sauce, light soy sauce, and white pepper to taste. The porridge should be savory and well-seasoned.
  6. Ladle into bowls and garnish generously with chopped green onions and fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use day-old rice for the best texture - fresh rice can become too mushy and won't hold its shape as well in the porridge
  • Don't rush the simmering process; let the rice slowly break down to achieve that perfect creamy yet slightly chunky consistency
  • Taste and adjust seasoning at the end - the saltiness should be pronounced since rice absorbs a lot of flavor
