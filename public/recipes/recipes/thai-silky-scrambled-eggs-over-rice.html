Thai Silky Scrambled Eggs Over Rice — The Ultimate Comfort Food

⏱ 10 min 🍽 Serves 2 📊 Easy

There's something magical about perfectly cooked Thai-style scrambled eggs that transforms a simple bowl of rice into pure comfort. This technique, passed down through generations of Thai home cooks, creates eggs so silky and creamy they're almost custard-like in texture.

💡 This humble dish, known as 'kai krata' in Thai, is the ultimate comfort food that every Thai person grew up eating. The key to achieving that signature silky texture lies in cooking the eggs over the gentlest heat possible and removing them from the pan while they still look underdone.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice, warmed
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl and whisk gently with salt and white pepper until just combined. Don't overbeat as this can make the eggs tough.
  2. Heat oil or butter in a non-stick pan over the lowest possible heat. The pan should be warm, not hot.
  3. Pour in the beaten eggs and immediately start stirring very slowly with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula. Move the eggs constantly in slow, gentle motions.
  4. Continue stirring for 3-4 minutes until the eggs form soft, creamy curds that still look slightly wet. Remove from heat while they still appear slightly underdone.
  5. Let the eggs sit in the warm pan for 30 seconds to finish cooking from residual heat. The texture should be silky and custard-like.
  6. Serve immediately over warm jasmine rice, garnish with chopped green onions and cilantro. Drizzle with light soy sauce if desired.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use the lowest heat setting on your stove - the eggs should cook so slowly that it takes several minutes to set
  • Stop cooking when the eggs still look slightly wet and glossy, as they'll continue cooking from the pan's residual heat
  • Fresh eggs at room temperature create the silkiest texture, so take them out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking
