Thai Pork Omelet (Kai Jeow Moo Sab) — Crispy-Edged Comfort Food

⏱ 10 min 🍽 Serves 2 📊 Easy

This isn't your ordinary omelet - kai jeow moo sab is Thailand's crispy-edged comfort food that transforms simple eggs into something magical. With ground pork folded into fluffy beaten eggs and deep-fried until golden and puffy, it's the ultimate quick meal served over steamed rice.

thai foodquick dinnercomfort foodporkomelet
💡 Kai jeow is Thailand's beloved everyday omelet, found in every home kitchen and street food stall. The secret to authentic kai jeow is using plenty of hot oil to create those signature puffy, crispy edges that Thai people adore.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 3.5 oz ground pork
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying (about 1 cup)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a bowl, crack the eggs and beat them well until completely combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Add the ground pork and fish sauce to the beaten eggs, mixing thoroughly to distribute the pork evenly throughout.
  3. Heat about 1 cup of oil in a wok or deep pan over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C) - the oil should be hot enough that a drop of egg mixture sizzles immediately.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the hot oil all at once. The omelet will puff up dramatically and the edges will become golden and crispy.
  5. Fry for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy, then carefully flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  6. Remove from oil and drain briefly on paper towels before serving immediately over steamed jasmine rice.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use plenty of oil - don't be afraid! The high oil temperature creates the characteristic puffed, crispy texture that makes Thai omelets special.
  • Beat the eggs vigorously until frothy to incorporate air, which helps create the light, fluffy interior.
  • Serve immediately while the edges are still crispy - kai jeow is best enjoyed fresh and hot from the pan.
