Durian Coconut Bread — Fluffy Vegan Thai Sweet Bread for Durian Lovers

⏱ 55 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

If you're a durian lover, this soft and fragrant Thai durian bread will become your new obsession. Made with creamy coconut milk and sweet durian flesh, this vegan-friendly loaf captures the essence of Thailand's king of fruits in every fluffy bite.

💡 This vegan durian bread celebrates Thailand's beloved 'king of fruits' in a soft, pillowy loaf that's perfect with afternoon tea. Durian's creamy, custard-like texture pairs beautifully with coconut milk, creating a uniquely Thai flavor combination that durian enthusiasts will absolutely adore.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 9 oz fresh durian flesh, mashed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Mix well to distribute evenly.
  2. Create a well in the center and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Turn dough onto a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, mash the durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until smooth. Set aside.
  6. Punch down the risen dough and roll into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll the dough tightly from the long side, pinching the seam to seal. Place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise for another 30-45 minutes until the dough crowns the pan. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  10. Brush the top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.
  11. Cool in pan for 10 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack. Let cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ripe but firm durian flesh — overly ripe durian can make the bread too wet and heavy
  • Don't skip the second rise; it's crucial for achieving that signature fluffy texture Thai bakeries are known for
  • Wrap leftover bread tightly and store at room temperature for up to 3 days, or freeze slices for longer storage
