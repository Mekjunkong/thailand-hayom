Thai Sandwich Rolls — Easy Make-Ahead Lunch Wraps

⏱ 15 min 🍽 Makes 4 rolls 📊 Easy

These Thai-style sandwich rolls are the perfect solution for neat, portable lunches that won't fall apart in your hands. Popular in Thai convenience stores and school cafeterias, they're incredibly easy to make and can be prepared ahead of time for busy weekdays.

💡 These sandwich rolls became popular in Thailand as a convenient, Western-influenced snack that's perfect for school lunch boxes and picnics. The tight rolling technique ensures they stay fresh and neat, making them ideal for on-the-go eating.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 slices thin white bread, crusts removed
  • 4 oz sliced ham or canned tuna, drained
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2-3 lettuce leaves, washed and dried
  • 1 medium tomato, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 oz cheddar cheese slices (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Using a rolling pin, gently flatten each slice of bread to make it more pliable for rolling.
  2. Spread a thin layer of mayonnaise on one side of each bread slice, leaving a small border around the edges.
  3. Layer the ham or tuna, cheese (if using), lettuce, tomato, and cucumber slices on each bread slice, keeping the filling toward one end.
  4. Starting from the filled end, tightly roll each sandwich into a log shape, pressing gently to seal.
  5. Wrap each roll individually in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set the shape.
  6. When ready to serve, unwrap and slice each roll into 3-4 pieces with a sharp knife.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Remove the crusts completely and flatten the bread well to prevent cracking when rolling
  • Don't overfill the sandwiches or they'll be difficult to roll and may fall apart
  • Chilling the wrapped rolls for at least 30 minutes helps them hold their shape when sliced
