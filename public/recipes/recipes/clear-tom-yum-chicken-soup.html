Clear Tom Yum Chicken Soup — Thailand's Warming Rainy Season Comfort Bowl

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

When Thailand's rainy season arrives, nothing beats a steaming bowl of clear tom yum chicken soup to warm both body and soul. This aromatic broth, fragrant with galangal, lemongrass, and lime leaves, represents the perfect marriage of Thai herbs that not only taste incredible but traditionally help keep you cozy during cool, damp weather.

💡 Tom yum nam sai (clear tom yum) is Thailand's beloved rainy season comfort food, traditionally enjoyed when the weather turns cool and damp. The aromatic herbs not only create incredible flavor but also provide warming properties that Thai people believe help maintain body heat and support respiratory health during the monsoon months.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 10.5 oz chicken (drumsticks or thighs), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3-4 slices fresh galangal
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, bruised and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 5-6 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 5-8 bird's eye chilies, lightly crushed
  • 1 cup straw mushrooms or oyster mushrooms, halved if large
  • 2 cups chicken stock or water
  • Fish sauce to taste (about 2-3 tablespoons)
  • Fresh lime juice to taste (about 2-3 tablespoons)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the chicken stock or water to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the galangal slices, lemongrass pieces, and kaffir lime leaves to the boiling liquid. Let simmer for 3-4 minutes to release the aromatic oils.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and cook for 8-10 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  4. Add the mushrooms and crushed chilies, then simmer for another 2-3 minutes until the mushrooms are tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce to taste, starting with 2 tablespoons and adjusting as needed.
  6. Remove from heat and add fresh lime juice to taste, starting with 2 tablespoons. The soup should have a perfect balance of sour, salty, and spicy flavors.
  7. Serve immediately in bowls while piping hot, garnished with additional lime wedges if desired.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't skip bruising the lemongrass — use the flat side of your knife to crush it, which releases maximum flavor into the broth
  • Add lime juice only after removing from heat to preserve its bright, fresh acidity and prevent it from becoming bitter
  • Taste and adjust the soup at the end — it should be predominantly sour and salty with a good kick of heat from the chilies
