Thai Beef Salad with Lemongrass — Fiery & Fresh Yam Neua That'll Stop You in Your Tracks

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

This isn't your average salad — yam neua is a flavor bomb that perfectly captures the essence of Thai cuisine in every bite. With tender beef, aromatic lemongrass, and a dressing that hits all the right notes, this dish will have you coming back for more before you've even finished your first serving.

thai foodbeef saladspicyfresh herbsauthentic thai
💡 Yam neua is a cornerstone of Thai cuisine, representing the perfect balance of hot, sour, salty, and sweet that defines authentic Thai cooking. The combination of fresh herbs and that punchy nam yam dressing creates a dish that's both cooling and warming at the same time.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or eye of round, sliced thin
  • 3 stalks lemongrass, tender parts only, finely sliced
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 6-8 kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • 3-4 Thai bird's eye chilies, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp palm sugar or brown sugar

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill or pan-sear the beef slices over high heat for 2-3 minutes per side until just cooked through but still tender. Let cool slightly, then slice against the grain into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic together until you have a rough paste. This releases all those essential oils that make Thai salads so aromatic.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the chili-garlic paste, fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar until the sugar dissolves completely.
  4. Add the warm beef to the dressing and toss well to coat. The slight warmth helps the meat absorb all those bold flavors.
  5. Add the sliced lemongrass, red onion, kaffir lime leaves, and mint. Toss everything together gently but thoroughly.
  6. Let the salad rest for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld, then taste and adjust seasoning with more lime juice, fish sauce, or sugar as needed. Serve immediately.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the beef — it should be just cooked through to stay tender and juicy in the salad
  • Use a mortar and pestle for the chili-garlic paste if possible; it creates a better texture than a food processor
  • Serve this salad at room temperature or slightly warm for the best flavor experience
