Japanese Cucumber with Miso Dip — A Refreshing Thai-Japanese Fusion Appetizer

⏱ 10 min 🍽 Serves 4 as an appetizer 📊 Easy

Sometimes the most elegant dishes are the simplest ones. This Japanese cucumber with miso dip brings together the clean, crisp bite of fresh cucumbers with the umami-rich sweetness of white miso sauce. It's a refreshing appetizer that takes just minutes to prepare but delivers sophisticated flavors that will impress your guests.

💡 This dish represents the beautiful fusion of Japanese ingredients with Thai presentation style that's become popular in modern Thai cuisine. Japanese cucumbers are prized for their crisp texture and mild flavor, making them perfect for this clean, refreshing appetizer.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 Japanese cucumbers (or Persian cucumbers)
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon toasted white sesame seeds

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the white miso paste, sugar, and mirin until smooth and well combined. Set the dipping sauce aside.
  2. Wash and dry the Japanese cucumbers. Cut them into thick diagonal slices about 1/2 inch thick, or cut lengthwise in half for easy dipping.
  3. Arrange the cucumber pieces on a serving plate. Serve alongside the miso dipping sauce and sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over everything for garnish.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Choose firm Japanese cucumbers without soft spots for the best crunch - they should feel heavy for their size
  • Make the miso sauce ahead of time and let it sit for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together
  • Toast your own sesame seeds in a dry pan for 2-3 minutes for maximum flavor and aroma
