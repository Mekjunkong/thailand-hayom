Spicy Thai Beef Salad (Yam Neua) — The Ultimate Refreshing Meat Lover's Feast

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

Nothing beats the incredible combination of tender grilled beef, crisp vegetables, and that addictive Thai dressing that hits all the right flavor notes. This yam neua (Thai beef salad) is the kind of dish that'll have you licking the bowl clean and immediately planning when you can make it again.

thai saladbeef recipespicy foodhealthy dinnerisaan cuisine
💡 Yam Neua is a beloved Thai salad that perfectly balances the four fundamental Thai flavors: sour, salty, sweet, and spicy. This dish originated in northeastern Thailand (Isaan region) and has become a staple across the country, especially during hot weather when its refreshing qualities are most appreciated.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 8 oz ribeye steak or sirloin, about 1-inch thick
  • 1 Japanese cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 8-10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 Japanese green onions (or regular scallions), chopped
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons toasted rice powder (optional but recommended)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill the beef over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness. Let it rest for 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
  2. While the beef rests, prepare the dressing by whisking together lime juice, fish sauce, palm sugar, minced chilies, and garlic until the sugar dissolves completely.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced beef, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and green onions. Pour the dressing over and toss gently to coat everything evenly.
  4. Sprinkle with toasted rice powder if using, and garnish with fresh cilantro and mint leaves. Serve immediately while the salad is cool and refreshing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Chill your cucumber slices in ice water for 10 minutes before mixing — this adds an extra crispy texture that makes the salad incredibly refreshing
  • Don't skip the resting time for your beef; it allows the juices to redistribute and prevents the meat from becoming tough when sliced
  • Make your own toasted rice powder by dry-frying jasmine rice until golden, then grinding it — it adds an authentic nutty flavor and slight crunch
