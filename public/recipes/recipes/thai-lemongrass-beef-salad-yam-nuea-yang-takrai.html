Thai Lemongrass Beef Salad (Yam Nuea Yang Takrai) — Fresh, Fiery & Fragrant

⏱ 30 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Medium

This vibrant Thai lemongrass beef salad is a celebration of fresh, bold flavors that will awaken every taste bud. The combination of tender grilled beef, aromatic lemongrass, and a punchy lime dressing creates the perfect balance of heat, sourness, and fragrance that makes Thai cuisine so irresistible.

💡 Yam salads are the soul of Thai cuisine, representing the perfect balance of hot, sour, salty, and sweet that defines authentic Thai flavors. This lemongrass beef version is particularly beloved in Northern and Northeastern Thailand, where fresh herbs are abundant and the preference for bold, refreshing flavors runs deep.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 8 oz beef sirloin or beef round, thinly sliced
  • 3 stalks fresh lemongrass, tender parts only, finely sliced
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 6-8 kaffir lime leaves, stems removed, finely shredded
  • 3-4 Thai bird's eye chilies (prik kee noo), or to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon palm sugar or brown sugar

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill or pan-sear the beef slices over high heat until cooked through but still tender, about 2-3 minutes per side. Set aside to cool slightly, then slice into bite-sized strips.
  2. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic together until you have a rough paste. If you don't have a mortar, finely mince them together with a knife.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chili-garlic paste with fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  4. Add the sliced beef to the dressing and toss well to coat. Let it marinate for 2-3 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  5. Add the lemongrass, red onion, kaffir lime leaves, and mint to the beef. Toss everything together gently but thoroughly.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with more lime juice, fish sauce, or chilies as needed. Serve immediately while fresh.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Slice the lemongrass as thinly as possible and use only the tender inner parts — the outer layers can be tough and fibrous
  • Don't skip the marinating step after adding the dressing to the beef; those few minutes allow the meat to absorb the flavors properly
  • Serve this salad at room temperature or slightly warm for the best flavor experience — never straight from the refrigerator
