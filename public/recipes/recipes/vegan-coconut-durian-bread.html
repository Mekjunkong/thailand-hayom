Vegan Coconut Durian Bread — Fluffy Thai Sweet Bread for Durian Lovers

⏱ 180 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

If you're a durian enthusiast, this fluffy vegan coconut durian bread will become your new obsession. This Thai-inspired sweet bread transforms the king of fruits into a pillowy soft loaf that's perfumed with coconut milk and just sweet enough to let durian's unique flavor shine through.

💡 This vegan durian bread celebrates Thailand's beloved 'king of fruits' in a soft, pillowy loaf that's perfect for afternoon tea. Durian's creamy, custard-like texture pairs beautifully with coconut milk, creating a uniquely Thai flavor combination that's both comforting and exotic.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 9 oz fresh durian flesh, mashed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Mix well to distribute the yeast evenly.
  2. Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, mash the durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until smooth. Set aside for filling.
  6. Punch down the risen dough and roll it out into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll up the dough tightly from the long side, pinching the seam to seal. Place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise again for 45 minutes until the dough crowns the pan. Preheat oven to 350°F during the last 15 minutes.
  10. Brush the top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and hollow-sounding when tapped.
  11. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ripe but not overripe durian for the best texture — it should be creamy but not mushy or overly pungent
  • Don't skip the second rise as it's crucial for achieving that signature fluffy texture Thai bakery breads are known for
  • If you can't find fresh durian, frozen durian flesh works well — just thaw completely and drain excess liquid before mashing
