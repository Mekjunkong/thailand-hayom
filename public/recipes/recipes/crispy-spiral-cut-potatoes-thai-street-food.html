Crispy Spiral Cut Potatoes — Thai Street Food Gold

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Easy

If you've ever wandered through Thai street markets, you've probably seen vendors expertly spiraling potatoes into golden, crispy masterpieces that look almost too pretty to eat. These spiral-cut potatoes are pure street food magic - simple ingredients transformed into an irresistibly crunchy snack that disappears faster than you can make it.

💡 These spiral-cut potatoes are a popular Thai street snack that showcases the creativity of Thai vendors who turn simple ingredients into Instagram-worthy treats. The spiral cut maximizes surface area for ultimate crispiness, while the seasoning powder adds that distinctive Thai street food flavor that keeps you coming back for more.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 medium russet potatoes (about 2 pounds)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying (about 4-6 cups)
  • 2 teaspoons seasoning powder (chicken, BBQ, or your favorite flavor)
  • Pinch of salt

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Wash and peel the potatoes, then pat them completely dry with paper towels.
  2. Using a sharp knife or spiral cutter, carefully cut each potato into a continuous spiral, keeping the cuts about 1/4 inch apart. The goal is to create one long spiral per potato that stays connected.
  3. Gently stretch each spiral to create gaps between the coils, allowing for even cooking and maximum crispiness.
  4. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot or wok. The oil should be deep enough to fully submerge the potato spirals.
  5. Carefully lower 1-2 potato spirals into the hot oil and fry for 3-4 minutes until golden brown and crispy, turning gently if needed.
  6. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels for 30 seconds, then immediately sprinkle with seasoning powder and a pinch of salt while still hot.
  7. Serve immediately while crispy and warm for the best texture and flavor.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Keep the potato spirals in cold water after cutting to prevent browning, but make sure to dry them thoroughly before frying to avoid oil splatter
  • Don't overcrowd the oil when frying - this drops the temperature and results in soggy potatoes instead of crispy ones
  • Season immediately after frying while the potatoes are still hot and slightly oily so the seasoning sticks better
