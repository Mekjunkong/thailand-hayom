Thai Bacon-Wrapped Enoki Mushrooms — Crispy Grilled Street Food Perfection

⏱ 23 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 as appetizer 📊 Easy

These bacon-wrapped enoki mushrooms are pure street food magic — crispy, smoky bacon hugging tender, earthy mushrooms in perfect harmony. Popular at Thai night markets, they're surprisingly simple to make at home and guaranteed to disappear as soon as they hit the table.

💡 This popular Thai street food snack combines the umami-rich enoki mushrooms with crispy bacon for an irresistible bite. You'll find these sizzling on grills at night markets across Thailand, where the smoky aroma draws crowds of hungry locals and tourists alike.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 7 oz fresh enoki mushrooms (golden needle mushrooms)
  • 10 slices bacon, thinly sliced
  • 10 wooden toothpicks or small skewers
  • Ketchup or soy sauce for dipping

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Divide the enoki mushrooms into 10 small bundles, each about the size of your thumb. Trim off any tough root ends.
  2. Wrap each mushroom bundle tightly with a slice of bacon, starting from the bottom and spiraling up. Secure with a toothpick through the center.
  3. Heat a dry skillet or grill pan over medium heat. Place the bacon-wrapped mushrooms seam-side down first.
  4. Cook for 6-8 minutes total, turning every 2 minutes until the bacon is golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  5. Serve immediately while hot with ketchup or soy sauce for dipping.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcrowd the pan — cook in batches if needed to ensure even browning and crispy bacon
  • Choose bacon that's not too thick, as it needs to cook through while the delicate mushrooms stay tender
  • For extra Thai flavor, try dipping in sweet chili sauce or nam jim seafood sauce instead of ketchup
