Thai-Style Garlic Cheese Toast — Crispy Street Food Comfort

⏱ 10 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

There's something magical about the sizzle of garlic butter hitting a hot pan and the sight of cheese melting into golden perfection. This Thai-style garlic cheese toast brings together the best of Western comfort food with that distinctive Thai street food flair that makes everything taste just a little bit better.

thai street foodgarlic breadcomfort foodquick snackcheese toast
💡 This Thai-style garlic cheese toast is a beloved street food snack that perfectly combines Western bread with Thai flavors. You'll find vendors selling these golden, crispy treats from food carts throughout Bangkok, especially in the evening when the aroma draws hungry passersby.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4-5 thick slices French bread or sourdough, cut 3/4-inch thick
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1-2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (to taste)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Lightly butter the surface of the pan.
  2. Mix the softened butter with minced garlic and parsley in a small bowl until well combined.
  3. Spread the garlic butter mixture generously on one side of each bread slice.
  4. Place the bread butter-side down in the heated pan and cook until golden and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese over half of each bread slice while still in the pan.
  6. Fold the bread in half to create a sandwich, gently pressing down to help the cheese melt.
  7. Continue cooking until both sides are golden brown and the cheese is fully melted, flipping carefully as needed.
  8. Remove from heat, cut diagonally into triangles, and serve immediately while hot and crispy.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use day-old bread for the best texture — it won't get soggy and will crisp up beautifully
  • Don't skip the parsley as it adds a fresh brightness that balances the rich garlic butter
  • Keep the heat at medium to ensure the bread gets crispy without burning before the cheese melts
