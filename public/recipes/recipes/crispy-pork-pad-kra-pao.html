Crispy Pork Pad Kra Pao — The Ultimate Thai Comfort Food

⏱ 18 min 🍽 Serves 2 📊 Easy

When Thais crave comfort food, they reach for pad kra pao — and the crispy pork version is pure indulgence. This isn't your average stir-fry; it's a fragrant, fiery dish that balances the richness of crispy pork with the peppery bite of holy basil and chilies.

💡 Pad kra pao is Thailand's ultimate comfort food, found at every street corner and office cafeteria. The key is using holy basil (kra pao), not sweet basil, which gives this dish its distinctive peppery, almost clove-like aroma that defines authentic Thai flavor.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 7 oz crispy pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 5-8 bird's eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 1 large handful fresh holy basil leaves (about 1 cup packed)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies together into a coarse paste. If you don't have a mortar, roughly chop them together with a knife.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking. Add the garlic-chili paste and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the crispy pork pieces and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, breaking up any large chunks and letting the pork get heated through.
  4. Add oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and fish sauce. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until everything is well coated.
  5. Turn off the heat and immediately add the holy basil leaves. Toss quickly until the basil wilts from the residual heat.
  6. Serve immediately over steamed jasmine rice with a fried egg on top for the complete experience.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Holy basil is crucial — it has a spicier, more complex flavor than sweet basil. Look for it at Asian markets or grow your own.
  • Don't skip the mortar and pestle for the garlic and chilies. The bruising action releases oils that chopping simply can't match.
  • Add the basil at the very end with the heat off to preserve its aromatic oils and prevent it from turning black.
