Korean-Style Corn Dogs (Thai Street Food Twist) — Crispy, Cheesy, and Irresistible

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Makes 4 corn dogs 📊 Easy

Korean corn dogs have taken Thailand's street food scene by storm, and now you can make these crispy, cheesy delights at home! With their golden breadcrumb coating and gooey mozzarella center, they're the perfect fusion of Korean innovation and Thai street food mastery.

korean foodstreet foodfried snackscheesethai fusion
💡 Korean corn dogs became a massive hit in Thailand's street food scene, blending K-pop culture with local snacking habits. Thai vendors often add their own twist with unique dipping sauces and extra-crispy coatings that make them irresistibly crunchy.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 mozzarella cheese sticks or sausages
  • 1 package Korean corn dog batter mix (about 7 oz)
  • 1 cup coarse breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying
  • Mustard sauce for serving
  • Mayonnaise for serving
  • 4 wooden skewers

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Insert wooden skewers into the mozzarella sticks or sausages, leaving about 3 inches of skewer exposed for handling.
  2. Prepare the corn dog batter according to package instructions, mixing until smooth and thick enough to coat.
  3. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot or fryer. The oil should be deep enough to fully submerge the corn dogs.
  4. Dip each skewered item into the batter, coating completely, then roll immediately in coarse breadcrumbs for extra crunch.
  5. Carefully lower the coated corn dogs into the hot oil and fry for 3-4 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels for 1 minute before serving hot with mustard and mayonnaise.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Make sure your oil temperature stays consistent at 350°F — too hot and the outside burns before the cheese melts, too cool and they'll be greasy
  • Pat the mozzarella sticks completely dry before coating to help the batter stick better and prevent oil splatter
  • For extra flavor, try mixing a pinch of garlic powder or paprika into your breadcrumbs before rolling
