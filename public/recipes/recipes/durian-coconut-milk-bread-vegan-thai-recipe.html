Durian Coconut Milk Bread — Soft Vegan Thai Sweet Bread Recipe

⏱ 240 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

This fragrant Thai durian bread transforms the polarizing 'king of fruits' into something magical. With coconut milk creating an incredibly soft crumb and sweet durian filling throughout, even durian skeptics find themselves reaching for another slice of this bakery-style vegan treat.

💡 Durian bread represents Thailand's love affair with the controversial 'king of fruits.' While durian's pungent aroma can be polarizing, when baked into soft bread with coconut milk, it transforms into a sweet, custard-like flavor that even skeptics often enjoy.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour (450g)
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast (8g)
  • 2 teaspoons salt (20g)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (30g)
  • 1 cup coconut milk (250ml)
  • 1/2 cup cold water (100ml)
  • 1 cup pureed durian flesh (250g)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Mix the dry ingredients thoroughly.
  2. Create a well in the center and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, mix pureed durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined. Set aside.
  6. Punch down risen dough and roll into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll up tightly from the long side, pinch seams to seal, and place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise for another 45-60 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  10. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using.
  11. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C).
  12. Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ripe but not overripe durian for the best texture - it should be creamy but not too liquidy
  • The coconut milk should be at room temperature to prevent shocking the yeast
  • Don't skip the second rise - it's crucial for achieving that pillowy soft texture Thai bakeries are famous for
