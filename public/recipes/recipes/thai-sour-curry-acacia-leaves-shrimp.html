Thai Sour Curry with Acacia Leaves and Shrimp — Gaeng Som Chaom Khai Goong

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

This traditional Thai sour curry brings together the unique flavors of acacia leaves, tender shrimp, and silky egg ribbons in a gloriously tangy broth. It's the kind of soul-warming dish Thai families turn to during rainy season, when the bright, sour flavors provide the perfect antidote to heavy, humid air.

💡 Gaeng som is a beloved rainy season comfort food in Thailand, known for its bright, tangy flavor that cuts through humid weather. Acacia leaves (cha-om) have a distinctive slightly bitter taste that pairs beautifully with the sour curry base, making this dish a true taste of authentic Thai home cooking.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh acacia leaves (cha-om), about 4 oz
  • 2 large eggs
  • 5 oz fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons sour curry paste (nam prik gaeng som) or store-bought
  • 2 tablespoons wet tamarind paste
  • Palm sugar to taste
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • 4 cups water

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean the acacia leaves thoroughly and remove any tough stems. Beat the eggs lightly in a small bowl.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add the sour curry paste and stir until completely dissolved.
  3. Add the wet tamarind paste to the pot and stir well. Let it simmer for 3-4 minutes to develop the sour base.
  4. Add the shrimp to the simmering curry and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and are almost cooked through.
  5. Gently add the acacia leaves to the pot and cook for 2 minutes until they're tender but still bright green.
  6. Slowly drizzle the beaten eggs into the curry while stirring gently to create silky egg ribbons throughout the soup.
  7. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar to taste, balancing the sour, salty, and slightly sweet flavors. Simmer for another minute and serve immediately with steamed rice.

💡 Pro Tips

  • If you can't find fresh acacia leaves, look for them frozen at Asian grocery stores — they work just as well and retain their unique flavor
  • The key to perfect egg ribbons is to drizzle the beaten eggs very slowly while stirring the curry gently in one direction
  • Taste and adjust the tamarind, fish sauce, and palm sugar at the end — the perfect gaeng som should be primarily sour with a hint of saltiness and sweetness
