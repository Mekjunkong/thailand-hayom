Thai Grilled Beef Salad with Ponzu — Refreshing Fusion Yam Neua

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

When Thai yam meets Japanese ponzu, magic happens on your plate. This fusion grilled beef salad combines the best of both worlds — Thailand's masterful balance of flavors with Japan's clean, umami-rich ponzu sauce.

thai fusionbeef saladponzugrilled meatrefreshing dinner
💡 This dish represents the beautiful fusion of Thai yam (salad) techniques with Japanese flavors. The ponzu adds a citrusy umami depth that perfectly complements the traditional Thai approach of balancing hot, sour, salty, and sweet.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb ribeye or sirloin steak
  • 1 Japanese cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 Japanese green onions (negi), sliced diagonally
  • 3 tablespoons ponzu sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 Thai bird's eye chili, minced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill the beef over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness. Let rest for 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
  2. While beef rests, chill the cucumber slices in ice water for extra crispness. Drain and pat dry before using.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together ponzu sauce, lime juice, sugar, and minced chili if using.
  4. Add the sliced beef, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and green onions to the dressing. Toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve immediately while the beef is still slightly warm, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Chill your cucumber slices in ice water for 10 minutes before mixing — this creates an incredibly refreshing contrast to the warm beef
  • Don't overdress the salad; the ponzu should enhance, not overpower the beef's natural flavors
  • Serve on chilled plates to maintain that refreshing temperature contrast that makes this dish so special
