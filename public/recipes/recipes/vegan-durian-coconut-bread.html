Vegan Durian Coconut Bread — Fluffy Thai Sweet Bread with the King of Fruits

⏱ 180 min 🍽 Makes 1 large loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

If you're a durian lover, this vegan durian coconut bread will become your new obsession. This pillowy-soft Thai-style sweet bread transforms the king of fruits into an irresistible loaf that's perfect for breakfast or an afternoon treat with Thai tea.

💡 Durian bread is a beloved Thai bakery staple that transforms the polarizing 'king of fruits' into something even durian skeptics might enjoy. The baking process mellows durian's intense aroma while preserving its rich, custardy flavor that Thais absolutely adore.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 9 oz durian flesh, blended smooth
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined.
  2. Create a well in the center and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, mix the blended durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined. Set aside.
  6. Once risen, punch down dough and roll into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll up tightly from the long side, pinch seams to seal, and place seam-side down in a greased 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise for another 45 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  10. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Sprinkle sliced almonds on top if using.
  11. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C).
  12. Cool in pan for 10 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use frozen durian flesh if fresh isn't available - thaw completely and drain excess liquid before blending for best texture
  • The dough will be slightly stickier than regular bread due to the coconut milk, but resist adding too much flour as this keeps the bread tender
  • Let the bread cool completely before slicing - the durian filling needs time to set properly for clean, beautiful slices
