Vegan Durian Coconut Bread — Fluffy Thai Sweet Bread for Durian Lovers

⏱ 200 min 🍽 Makes 1 large loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

If you're part of the durian-loving club, this fragrant Thai bread will become your new obsession. Soft, pillowy dough infused with coconut milk cradles sweet, creamy durian filling in every bite. This vegan version proves you don't need eggs or dairy to create bakery-worthy results at home.

💡 Durian bread is a beloved Thai bakery staple that celebrates our 'king of fruits.' This vegan version maintains all the rich, custardy flavor durian lovers crave while being accessible to plant-based eaters. The coconut milk adds traditional Thai aromatics and keeps the bread incredibly moist.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 9 oz fresh durian flesh, mashed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Mix well to distribute the yeast evenly.
  2. Create a well in the center and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, mash the durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until smooth. Set aside.
  6. Punch down the risen dough and roll out into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll up the dough tightly from the long side, pinching the seam to seal. Place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise again for 45-60 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  10. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using.
  11. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C).
  12. Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Choose ripe durian with a strong aroma but firm texture - overly soft durian will make the bread too wet
  • If the dough feels too sticky during kneading, add flour 1 tablespoon at a time rather than all at once
  • Let the bread cool completely before slicing to prevent the durian filling from oozing out
