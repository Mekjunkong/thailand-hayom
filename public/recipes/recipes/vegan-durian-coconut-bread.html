Vegan Durian Coconut Bread — Fluffy Thai Sweet Bread with the King of Fruits

⏱ 180 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

This vegan durian coconut bread brings the bold, creamy flavors of Thailand's most famous fruit into a soft, pillowy loaf that's perfect for adventurous bakers. The combination of rich coconut milk and sweet durian creates a uniquely Thai treat that's surprisingly approachable, even for those new to the king of fruits.

💡 Durian bread is a beloved Thai bakery staple that showcases Thailand's famous 'king of fruits.' While durian's pungent aroma can be polarizing, its creamy, custard-like flesh creates an incredibly rich and aromatic bread that's cherished by durian lovers throughout Southeast Asia.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 9 oz fresh durian flesh, mashed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix coconut milk and cold water. Gradually add the liquid mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  4. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the durian filling by mashing the durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until smooth. Set aside.
  6. Punch down the risen dough and roll it out into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  7. Spread the durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  8. Roll up the dough tightly from the long side, pinch the seam to seal, and place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  9. Cover and let rise for another 45 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  10. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Sprinkle sliced almonds on top if using.
  11. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C).
  12. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ripe but not overripe durian for the best texture - it should be creamy but not mushy or overly pungent.
  • The coconut milk adds moisture and a subtle tropical flavor that complements the durian beautifully - don't skip it!
  • Let the bread cool completely before slicing to prevent the soft crumb from compressing and to allow the durian flavors to fully develop.
