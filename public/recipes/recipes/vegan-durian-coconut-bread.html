Vegan Durian Coconut Bread — Soft, Fragrant Thai-Style Sweet Bread

⏱ 180 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

If you're among the devoted fans of durian, Thailand's infamous 'king of fruits,' this vegan coconut bread will be your new obsession. The rich, custard-like durian flesh swirled through tender coconut milk bread creates an incredibly aromatic and satisfying treat that captures the essence of Thai bakery favorites.

💡 Durian bread is a beloved Thai bakery staple that celebrates Thailand's most polarizing fruit. This vegan version maintains all the rich, custardy flavor durian lovers crave while using coconut milk to create an incredibly tender crumb that's distinctly Southeast Asian.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 1 cup fresh durian flesh, mashed
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian filling)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Mix well to distribute the dry ingredients evenly.
  2. Create a well in the center and pour in coconut milk and cold water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then knead on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  4. Meanwhile, mash the durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined. Set aside for filling.
  5. Punch down the risen dough and roll it out into a rectangle about 12x8 inches on a floured surface.
  6. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  7. Roll up the dough tightly from the long side, pinch the seam to seal, and place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  8. Cover and let rise for another 45 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  9. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using.
  10. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C). Cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use very ripe durian for the sweetest flavor and smoothest texture — it should be soft enough to mash easily with a fork
  • Don't skip the second rise as it's crucial for achieving that signature soft, pillowy texture Thai bakeries are known for
  • Store leftover bread wrapped tightly as the durian aroma will intensify over time, making it even more fragrant the next day
