Homemade Korean Rice Cakes (Tteok) — Chewy Perfection from Scratch

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Makes about 20 rice cakes 📊 Medium

These homemade Korean rice cakes (tteok) bring the authentic chewy texture that makes tteokbokki and other Korean dishes so irresistible. With just a few simple ingredients and some patience, you can create these pillowy-soft rice cakes that are far superior to store-bought versions.

💡 While this recipe creates Korean-style rice cakes (tteok), it's been adapted for Thai home cooks who love Korean cuisine. These chewy rice cakes are essential for making authentic tteokbokki and other Korean dishes that have become incredibly popular in Thailand's Korean food scene.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1⅔ cups glutinous rice flour (sweet rice flour)
  • ⅔ cup regular rice flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • Warm water as needed for kneading

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour, regular rice flour, and salt. Mix well to distribute the salt evenly.
  2. Add the vegetable oil to the flour mixture and mix with your hands or a wooden spoon.
  3. Gradually add warm water, a little at a time, while mixing and kneading the dough. You'll need about ½ to ¾ cup of water total.
  4. Knead the dough for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky. The dough should feel soft and pliable.
  5. Roll the dough into long cylinders about ½ inch thick, then cut into 2-inch pieces to form the classic rice cake shape.
  6. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Carefully add the rice cakes and cook for 8-10 minutes until they float to the surface.
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and rinse briefly in cold water to stop the cooking process. Use immediately or store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

💡 Pro Tips

  • The dough consistency is crucial — it should be soft and pliable but not sticky. Add water gradually to achieve the perfect texture.
  • Fresh homemade rice cakes have the best chewy texture, but you can freeze them for up to 3 months if needed.
  • If the rice cakes become hard after refrigerating, simply blanch them in boiling water for 1-2 minutes to restore their soft texture.
