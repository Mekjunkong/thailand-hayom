Homemade Ramen with Long Pork Dumplings in Rich Chicken Broth

⏱ 420 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Hard

This labor-intensive but incredibly rewarding ramen combines the best of Thai home cooking techniques with Japanese-inspired presentation. The result is a bowl of pure comfort featuring silky long pork dumplings, apple-sweetened chicken broth, and perfectly braised chicken wings that will make you forget about restaurant ramen forever.

💡 This elaborate ramen dish represents the Thai love for adapting international cuisines with local techniques. The long-shaped dumplings are a creative twist that makes slurping more enjoyable, while the apple-sweetened chicken broth reflects Thai preferences for natural sweetness in savory dishes.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup clean water
  • 1 tsp alkaline water (kansui) or baking soda solution
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 lb ground pork, seasoned and chilled overnight
  • 20 square dumpling wrappers, homemade
  • 2 lbs chicken bones and drumsticks
  • 1 medium apple, quartered
  • 1 large yellow onion, quartered
  • 2 tbsp rendered chicken fat
  • 8 chicken wings
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp thick soy sauce
  • 1 tsp dashi powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp mirin or sake (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Make the ramen noodles by mixing flour, water, alkaline water, and egg until a firm dough forms. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth, then rest for 30 minutes covered.
  2. Roll the dough thin and cut into ramen-style noodles. Dust with flour and set aside.
  3. For the dumpling filling, mix the seasoned ground pork thoroughly and chill overnight to set the texture.
  4. Roll dumpling wrappers slightly longer than usual and fill with pork mixture. Seal into elongated dumpling shapes.
  5. Prepare the chicken broth by placing chicken bones, drumsticks, apple, and onion in a large pot. Cover with water and simmer for 3-4 hours, skimming foam regularly.
  6. Strain the broth and reserve the rendered chicken fat that rises to the surface.
  7. For the braised chicken wings, combine fish sauce, soy sauce, thick soy sauce, dashi powder, sugar, and mirin in a pot.
  8. Add chicken wings to the sauce mixture and braise over low heat for 45 minutes until tender and well-glazed.
  9. Cook the ramen noodles in boiling water for 2-3 minutes until al dente. Cook the long dumplings in the same water for 5-6 minutes.
  10. Assemble bowls with hot noodles, long dumplings, ladle hot chicken broth over top, and garnish with braised chicken wings and a drizzle of chicken fat.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Chill the seasoned pork filling overnight - this helps it hold together better when wrapping and creates the perfect texture
  • Save the chicken fat that rises during broth making - it adds incredible richness when drizzled on the finished bowl
  • Make the dumpling wrappers longer than usual so they create fun, slurpable 'tails' in the soup
