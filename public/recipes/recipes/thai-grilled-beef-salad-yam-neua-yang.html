Thai Grilled Beef Salad (Yam Neua Yang) — The Ultimate Spicy Street Food Classic

⏱ 30 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

This authentic Thai grilled beef salad delivers an explosion of flavors that will transport you straight to a bustling Bangkok street market. The combination of perfectly grilled beef, fresh herbs, and that signature tangy-spicy dressing creates a dish that's both refreshing and satisfying.

thai saladgrilled beefspicy foodhealthy dinnerstreet food
💡 Yam neua yang is a beloved Thai salad that perfectly balances the four fundamental Thai flavors: sour, salty, sweet, and spicy. This dish originated in northeastern Thailand (Isaan) and has become a staple at Thai barbecue restaurants and street food stalls throughout the country.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 10 oz beef sirloin or ribeye, sliced into strips
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-5 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced
  • 1/2 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced diagonally
  • 3 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 cucumber, sliced

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Season the beef strips with salt and black pepper, then let marinate for 10 minutes at room temperature.
  2. Heat a grill pan or outdoor grill to medium-high heat and grill the beef for 2-3 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness. Let rest for 2 minutes, then slice into bite-sized pieces.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together lime juice, fish sauce, and sugar until the sugar completely dissolves.
  4. Add the minced garlic and chilies to the dressing and mix well.
  5. Add the sliced onion, tomato wedges, celery, green onions, and cilantro to the bowl with the dressing.
  6. Add the warm grilled beef to the salad and toss everything together gently but thoroughly.
  7. Serve immediately on a platter garnished with cucumber slices while the beef is still warm.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the beef — medium-rare to medium is ideal as the meat will continue to cook slightly when mixed with the warm dressing
  • Use the beef while it's still warm for the best texture and flavor absorption from the dressing
  • Adjust the chilies to your heat preference, but remember that the lime juice and fresh herbs help balance the spice
