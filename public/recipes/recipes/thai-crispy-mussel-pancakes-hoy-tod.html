Thai-Style Crispy Mussel Pancakes (Hoy Tod) — Street Food Gold at Home

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Medium

Nothing beats the sizzling sound of hoy tod hitting a hot wok at your favorite Thai street stall. These crispy mussel pancakes combine tender seafood with a golden, crunchy exterior that'll transport you straight to the bustling streets of Bangkok with every bite.

thai street foodseafoodcrispy pancakesmusselsauthentic thai
💡 Hoy tod is a beloved Thai street food that originated in coastal areas where fresh mussels were abundant. The contrast between the crispy exterior and tender mussels inside makes this dish irresistible to locals and visitors alike.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb fresh New Zealand green mussels, cleaned and shucked
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons tempura flour or all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying
  • 1 cup fresh bean sprouts
  • Mixed salad greens for garnish
  • Sweet chili sauce for dipping
  • Sriracha sauce for dipping

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean the mussels thoroughly under cold running water and remove from shells. Pat dry with paper towels and set aside.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together both flours, chicken bouillon powder, and white pepper. Gradually add ice-cold water while whisking to create a smooth, thin batter.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. The oil should be about 1/2 inch deep.
  4. Dip the mussels in the batter, allowing excess to drip off, then carefully place them in the hot oil. Fry until golden and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  5. Push the fried mussels to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Let them set slightly, then scramble and mix with the mussels.
  6. Add bean sprouts to the pan and toss everything together for 1-2 minutes until sprouts are just wilted but still crunchy.
  7. Transfer to a serving plate, garnish with fresh salad greens, and serve immediately with sweet chili sauce and sriracha for dipping.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ice-cold water in your batter - this creates a lighter, crispier texture when fried
  • Don't overcrowd the pan when frying; work in batches to maintain oil temperature and crispiness
  • Serve immediately while hot - the contrast between crispy pancake and tender mussels is best enjoyed fresh from the wok
