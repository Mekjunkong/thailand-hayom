Thai Silky Scrambled Eggs (Kai Khem) — Perfectly Creamy Comfort Food

⏱ 13 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

When Thai cooks want ultimate comfort food, they turn to kai khem - silky scrambled eggs cooked so slowly and gently they become like edible velvet. This technique transforms simple eggs into something magical, perfect for spooning over warm jasmine rice on a rainy day.

💡 This technique of slow-cooked scrambled eggs is a beloved Thai comfort food, especially popular during rainy season when people crave warm, soothing meals. The key is patience - Thai cooks know that the gentlest heat creates the silkiest texture that melts in your mouth.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons whole milk or heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice, warm
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon soy sauce for drizzling

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl and whisk gently with milk, salt, and white pepper until just combined. Don't overbeat - you want a smooth mixture without too much air.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan or well-seasoned wok over the lowest possible heat. Add butter and let it melt completely without browning.
  3. Pour in the egg mixture and let it sit for 30 seconds without stirring. Using a rubber spatula, gently push the eggs from the edges toward the center, tilting the pan to let uncooked egg flow underneath.
  4. Continue this gentle stirring motion every 30-45 seconds, always moving slowly. The eggs should cook very gradually, forming soft, creamy curds.
  5. When the eggs still look slightly wet and glossy (about 6-8 minutes total), remove from heat immediately. The residual heat will finish cooking them to perfect creaminess.
  6. Serve immediately over warm jasmine rice, garnished with green onions and cilantro. Drizzle with soy sauce if desired.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Stop cooking when eggs still look slightly underdone - they'll continue cooking from residual heat and achieve perfect creaminess
  • Use the lowest heat setting possible and resist the urge to rush - good kai khem takes time and patience
  • Fresh herbs like cilantro and green onions add brightness that balances the rich, creamy eggs perfectly
