Tom Saeb Pork Soft Bone — Spicy Thai Soup with Tender Cartilage

⏱ 60 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

Tom Saeb is one of Thailand's most intensely flavored soups, hailing from the northeastern Isaan region where bold, fiery tastes reign supreme. This rustic dish features tender pork soft bone simmered in an aromatic broth that's simultaneously spicy, sour, and deeply satisfying. The addition of toasted rice powder gives it a unique texture and nutty depth that sets it apart from other Thai soups.

💡 Tom Saeb is a rustic Isaan (northeastern Thai) soup known for its intense, fiery flavor profile. The soft bone cartilage provides a unique chewy texture that's prized in Thai cuisine, while the toasted rice powder adds body and a distinctive nutty taste that's characteristic of Isaan cooking.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 14 oz pork soft bone (cartilage), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 8-10 bird's eye chilies
  • 2 limes (juiced)
  • 2 tablespoons toasted rice powder
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1-inch piece galangal, sliced
  • 6-8 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar, or to taste
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves for garnish
  • 4 cups water or light pork stock

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water or pork stock to a rolling boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the pork soft bone pieces to the boiling water and cook for 30-35 minutes until tender but still with some bite to the cartilage.
  3. Add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves to the pot. Simmer for 5 minutes to release their aromatics.
  4. In a mortar and pestle, pound the bird's eye chilies until roughly crushed. Add to the soup for heat.
  5. Season the soup with fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting to taste. The flavor should be intensely spicy and slightly salty.
  6. Add the toasted rice powder and stir well. This will give the soup body and a nutty flavor.
  7. Remove from heat and stir in fresh lime juice just before serving.
  8. Ladle into bowls and garnish generously with fresh Thai basil leaves. Serve immediately while piping hot.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't skip the toasted rice powder — it's essential for authentic Tom Saeb flavor and helps thicken the broth naturally
  • The pork soft bone should be tender but still have some chew to it; overcooking will make it mushy and less appealing
  • Adjust the number of chilies based on your heat tolerance, but remember this soup is meant to be quite spicy
