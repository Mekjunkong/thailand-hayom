Thai-Style Mini Burgers — Crispy Patties with Melted Cheese

⏱ 18 min 🍽 Makes 4 mini burgers 📊 Easy

These Thai-style mini burgers bring together the best of comfort food in a perfectly sized package. With juicy beef patties topped with melted cheese and fresh vegetables, they're ideal for casual gatherings or when you want a satisfying meal without the commitment of a full-sized burger.

mini burgerseasy dinnercheese burgersthai fusionquick meals
💡 While burgers aren't traditionally Thai, this recipe reflects how Thai cooks have embraced international foods and made them their own. The emphasis on fresh vegetables and quality ingredients mirrors Thai cooking principles of balance and freshness.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1¼ lbs ground beef (about 5 oz per patty)
  • 4 mini burger buns, split
  • 4 slices cheese (cheddar or your preference)
  • Lettuce leaves, as desired
  • Cherry tomatoes, sliced, as desired
  • Mayonnaise, to taste
  • Mustard, to taste

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Shape the ground beef into 4 round balls, using about 5 oz of meat per patty. Gently flatten each ball into a patty shape, making sure not to overwork the meat.
  2. Heat a skillet or grill pan over high heat. Cook the patties for 3-4 minutes on each side, creating a nice sear on both sides.
  3. Place a slice of cheese on top of each patty during the last minute of cooking. Cover the pan with a lid to help the cheese melt completely.
  4. Warm the mini buns if desired. Assemble the burgers by spreading mayonnaise and mustard on the buns, then layering with lettuce, the cheesy beef patty, and tomato slices.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't press down on the patties while cooking — this squeezes out the juices and makes them dry
  • Make a small indent in the center of each raw patty to prevent them from puffing up during cooking
  • Let the meat rest at room temperature for 15 minutes before cooking for more even cooking
