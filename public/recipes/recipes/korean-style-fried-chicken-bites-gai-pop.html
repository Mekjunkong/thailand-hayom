Korean-Style Fried Chicken Bites (Gai Pop) — Crispy Thai-Korean Fusion

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 3-4 📊 Easy

Get ready for an addictive fusion of crispy Thai fried chicken meets Korean gochujang magic! This Gai Pop brings together the best of both worlds — Thailand's perfect frying technique and Korea's irresistible sweet-spicy flavors. It's the kind of dish that disappears from the plate before you know it, leaving everyone asking for the recipe.

💡 This dish represents the beautiful fusion of Thai and Korean flavors that's become incredibly popular in Thailand's modern food scene. The combination of crispy Thai-style fried chicken with Korea's beloved gochujang creates an irresistible sweet-spicy glaze that's both familiar and exciting.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 tablespoons crispy frying flour (tempura flour or all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon Korean soy sauce (or regular soy sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon white sesame seeds for garnish
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot or wok. Toss chicken pieces with crispy frying flour until evenly coated.
  2. Deep fry the coated chicken pieces for 5-7 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a large pan over medium heat, stir together gochujang, soy sauce, honey, and minced garlic until the sauce is smooth and fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  4. Add the fried chicken to the pan and toss quickly to coat all pieces with the sauce. Drizzle with sesame oil and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  5. Serve immediately while hot and crispy for the best texture and flavor.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't skip the sesame oil drizzle at the end — it adds an authentic Korean aroma that makes all the difference
  • Work quickly when tossing the chicken with sauce to maintain maximum crispiness
  • Double-fry the chicken if you want extra crunch: fry once at 325°F, rest for 2 minutes, then fry again at 350°F
