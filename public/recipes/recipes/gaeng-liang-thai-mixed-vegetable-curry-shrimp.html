Gaeng Liang — Thai Mixed Vegetable Curry with Fresh Shrimp

⏱ 45 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

Gaeng Liang is Thailand's ancient answer to comfort food - a light, peppery curry brimming with fresh vegetables and shrimp. Unlike its coconut milk-rich cousins, this traditional soup celebrates the pure flavors of the garden with an aromatic paste of shallots, shrimp paste, and white pepper.

thai curryhealthy soupvegetable curryauthentic thailight curry
💡 Gaeng Liang is one of Thailand's most ancient curries, traditionally made without coconut milk and packed with whatever vegetables were available. This humble dish represents the essence of Thai home cooking - simple, healthy, and deeply satisfying.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 medium bottle gourd (about 1 lb), peeled and cut into chunks
  • 7 oz pumpkin, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 5-6 baby corn ears, halved lengthwise
  • 5 oz fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 bunch Thai sweet basil leaves (bai maenglak)
  • 3 shallots, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste
  • 1 tsp white peppercorns
  • 4 cups water or light stock
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 Thai chilies, lightly crushed

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound shallots, shrimp paste, and white peppercorns into a coarse paste. This aromatic paste is the heart of gaeng liang's distinctive flavor.
  2. Bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add the pounded paste and stir well to dissolve completely.
  3. Add pumpkin pieces first as they take longest to cook. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until slightly tender.
  4. Add bottle gourd chunks and baby corn. Continue simmering for another 8-10 minutes until vegetables are tender but still hold their shape.
  5. Add fresh shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and are just cooked through.
  6. Season with fish sauce and tamarind paste. Taste and adjust - the soup should be mildly salty with a subtle sour note.
  7. Add crushed chilies for heat. Remove from heat and immediately stir in Thai sweet basil leaves, letting them wilt in the residual heat.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't skip the mortar and pestle for the paste - it creates the proper texture that releases maximum flavor from the aromatics
  • Add vegetables in order of cooking time needed, starting with the hardest ones first to ensure even cooking
  • The soup should have a clear, light broth with prominent pepper flavor - resist adding coconut milk to keep it authentic
