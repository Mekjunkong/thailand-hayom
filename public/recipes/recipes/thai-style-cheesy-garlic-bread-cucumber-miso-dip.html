Thai-Style Cheesy Garlic Bread with Japanese Cucumber Miso Dip

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

When Thai comfort food meets Japanese freshness, magic happens! This delightful combination of crispy, cheesy garlic bread paired with cool, tangy cucumber miso dip has become a favorite snack in Thailand's cafes and homes. It's the perfect balance of indulgent and refreshing - ideal for those times when you want something satisfying but not too heavy.

💡 This fusion snack combines the beloved Thai love for cheesy garlic bread with a refreshing Japanese-inspired cucumber dip. It's become popular in Thailand as a lighter alternative to heavy fried snacks, especially during sports viewing parties where people want something satisfying but not too heavy.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 6 slices thick white bread
  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 4 Japanese cucumbers
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon toasted white sesame seeds

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 355°F (180°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, minced garlic, and chopped parsley until well combined.
  3. Spread the garlic butter mixture evenly on one side of each bread slice.
  4. Generously sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the buttered bread.
  5. Place the bread on a baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes, until the cheese is melted and golden brown.
  6. While the bread is baking, slice the Japanese cucumbers into rounds or sticks.
  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the white miso paste, sugar, and mirin until smooth.
  8. Arrange the cucumber pieces on a plate and serve with the miso dipping sauce.
  9. Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over the cucumber and miso dip before serving.
  10. Serve the hot cheesy garlic bread alongside the cool cucumber miso dip for a perfect contrast.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use Japanese cucumbers if available - they're crunchier and have fewer seeds than regular cucumbers
  • Don't skip the mirin in the miso dip - it adds essential sweetness and depth that balances the salty miso
  • Serve the garlic bread immediately while hot and the cucumber dip chilled for the best temperature contrast
