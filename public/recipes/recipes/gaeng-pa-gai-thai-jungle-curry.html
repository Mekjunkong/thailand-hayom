Gaeng Pa Gai — Authentic Thai Jungle Curry with Chicken

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 3-4 📊 Medium

Gaeng Pa Gai is Thailand's boldest curry — a fiery, herbaceous jungle curry that skips the coconut milk in favor of pure, unadulterated spice and aromatics. This rustic dish from Thailand's forested regions showcases the wild flavors of krachai (wild ginger) and fresh green peppercorns in a broth so intensely flavorful, it'll transport you straight to a Thai village kitchen.

💡 Gaeng Pa, or jungle curry, originates from Thailand's forested regions where coconut milk wasn't readily available. This fiery, herbaceous curry relies on fresh jungle ingredients like wild ginger and green peppercorns for its distinctive flavor profile that's both rustic and intensely aromatic.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 10.5 oz chicken thighs or breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3-4 tablespoons jungle curry paste (nam prik gaeng pa)
  • 3-4 pieces wild ginger (krachai), sliced thin
  • 1 small handful green peppercorns (prik thai orn)
  • 10-12 Thai basil leaves (bai krapao)
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce, or to taste
  • 1-2 teaspoons palm sugar, or to taste
  • 1½ cups water or chicken stock

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat a wok or large pan over medium-high heat without oil. Add the jungle curry paste and dry-fry for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and the oils start to separate.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry with the curry paste for 3-4 minutes until the chicken is nearly cooked through and well coated.
  3. Pour in the water or stock gradually, stirring to combine with the curry paste. Bring to a gentle boil.
  4. Add the sliced wild ginger and green peppercorns. Simmer for 8-10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar, tasting and adjusting to balance salty, spicy, and slightly sweet flavors.
  6. In the final minute, stir in the torn kaffir lime leaves and Thai basil leaves until just wilted.
  7. Serve immediately with steamed jasmine rice while piping hot.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Dry-frying the curry paste first is crucial — it intensifies the flavors and prevents the curry from tasting raw or muddy
  • Wild ginger (krachai) has a unique piney, medicinal flavor that's irreplaceable, but if unavailable, use regular ginger with a pinch of white pepper
  • This curry should be quite soupy and very spicy — don't be tempted to add coconut milk as it would make it a different dish entirely
