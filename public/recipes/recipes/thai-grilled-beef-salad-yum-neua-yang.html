Thai Grilled Beef Salad (Yum Neua Yang) — The Ultimate Spicy & Tangy Thai Classic

⏱ 30 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

This authentic Thai grilled beef salad is pure magic — tender, perfectly seasoned beef tossed with a lip-smacking dressing that hits every flavor note. The secret is getting that perfect balance of lime juice, fish sauce, and sugar, then mixing it with the beef while it's still warm so every bite is incredibly flavorful.

thai saladgrilled beefspicy foodthai cuisineyum salad
💡 Yum neua yang is a beloved Thai salad that perfectly embodies the Thai philosophy of balancing sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors in one dish. The key is using the beef while it's still warm so it absorbs the vibrant dressing beautifully.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 10.5 oz beef sirloin or ribeye, sliced into strips
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-5 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced diagonally
  • 3 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced (for serving)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Season the beef strips with salt and black pepper, then let marinate for 10 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.
  2. Heat a grill pan or outdoor grill to medium-high heat. Grill the beef for 2-3 minutes per side for medium-rare, or longer if you prefer it more cooked.
  3. While the beef is still warm, slice it into bite-sized pieces against the grain.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together lime juice, fish sauce, and sugar until the sugar completely dissolves.
  5. Add the minced garlic and chilies to the dressing, then mix in the warm beef pieces.
  6. Add the sliced onion, tomato wedges, celery, green onions, and cilantro. Toss everything together gently but thoroughly.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with more lime juice, fish sauce, or sugar as needed. Serve immediately with cucumber slices and steamed sticky rice.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the beef — medium-rare to medium keeps it tender and juicy, especially when it absorbs the acidic dressing
  • Make sure your lime juice is fresh and the sugar completely dissolves in the dressing for the smoothest flavor balance
  • Serve immediately while the beef is still warm for the best texture and flavor absorption
