Crispy Thai Cheese Balls — Golden Fried Mozzarella Perfection

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Makes 12-15 cheese balls 📊 Easy

These golden, crispy cheese balls are Thailand's answer to the perfect party snack — crunchy on the outside with molten mozzarella that stretches beautifully when you bite in. Popular at Thai street markets and food courts, they're surprisingly easy to make at home and always disappear within minutes of serving.

💡 These crispy cheese balls became incredibly popular in Thailand's street food scene, often served at night markets and food courts. They're a perfect example of how Thai cuisine embraces international ingredients while making them uniquely Thai through cooking techniques.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 7 oz mozzarella cheese, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (for dredging)
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Cut the mozzarella cheese into uniform cubes, about 1-inch in size. Pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  2. Set up your breading station with three shallow bowls: flour in the first, beaten egg in the second, and panko breadcrumbs in the third.
  3. Dredge each cheese cube in flour, then dip in beaten egg, and finally coat thoroughly with panko breadcrumbs. Press gently to help the coating adhere.
  4. Place the coated cheese balls on a plate and refrigerate for 15 minutes to help the coating set and prevent the cheese from melting too quickly during frying.
  5. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot or wok. The oil should be deep enough to completely submerge the cheese balls.
  6. Carefully lower the cheese balls into the hot oil and fry for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Don't overcrowd the pot.
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve immediately while the cheese is still melted and gooey inside.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Chilling the breaded cheese balls is crucial — it prevents the cheese from completely melting out during frying
  • Make sure your oil temperature is accurate; too hot and the outside burns before the inside warms, too cool and they'll absorb too much oil
  • Serve immediately for the best experience — the contrast between the crispy exterior and molten cheese center is what makes these irresistible
