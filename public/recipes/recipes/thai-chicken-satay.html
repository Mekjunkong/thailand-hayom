Thai Chicken Satay — Grilled Street Food Perfection with Peanut Sauce

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

Nothing beats the aroma of chicken satay sizzling on the grill — it's pure Thai street food magic that you can easily recreate at home. This simple marinade of turmeric and coriander transforms ordinary chicken into something extraordinary, perfect for your next barbecue or weeknight dinner.

thai foodgrilled chickenstreet foodsataypeanut sauce
💡 Chicken satay is one of Thailand's most beloved street foods, originally influenced by Indonesian cuisine but made distinctly Thai with local spices and peanut sauce. You'll find satay vendors grilling these aromatic skewers over charcoal braziers throughout Thailand, filling the air with irresistible smoky aromas.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced into long strips (about 1/4 inch thick)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seed powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Sugar to taste
  • Vegetable oil for brushing
  • Store-bought or homemade peanut sauce for serving
  • 20-25 wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Thread the chicken strips onto the soaked wooden skewers, weaving them back and forth to secure. You should have about 20-25 skewers depending on the size of your chicken strips.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together turmeric powder, coriander powder, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of sugar. Rub this spice mixture all over the chicken skewers, coating evenly.
  3. Let the chicken marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to 2 hours in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
  4. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Brush the chicken lightly with oil to prevent sticking.
  5. Grill the satay for 2-3 minutes per side, turning once, until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks. The internal temperature should reach 165°F.
  6. Serve immediately while hot with peanut sauce on the side for dipping.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Soak your wooden skewers for at least 30 minutes before threading to prevent burning on the grill
  • Don't skip the marinating time — even 15 minutes makes a huge difference in flavor penetration
  • For authentic flavor, try grilling over charcoal if possible, as the smoky taste is part of what makes street-style satay so special
