Thai Grilled Beef Salad with Ponzu Dressing — A Fresh Fusion Delight

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

This vibrant Thai-Japanese fusion salad brings together the best of both culinary worlds - tender grilled beef meets crisp vegetables in a tangy ponzu dressing. It's the perfect light meal when you're craving something fresh and satisfying without the heaviness.

thai saladgrilled beeffusion cuisinehealthy dinnerponzu
💡 This dish represents the beautiful fusion of Thai and Japanese flavors that's become popular in modern Thai cuisine. The combination of Thai salad techniques with Japanese ponzu creates a light, refreshing meal perfect for hot weather.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb ribeye or sirloin steak
  • 1 large Japanese cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 Japanese scallions (negi), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 tablespoons ponzu sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Thai fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 Thai chili, minced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill the beef over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness. Let rest for 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
  2. While beef rests, chill the sliced cucumber in ice water for 10 minutes to maximize crispness, then drain well.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together ponzu sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and minced chili if using.
  4. Add the sliced beef, drained cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and scallions to the dressing. Toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve immediately while the salad is cool and refreshing, garnished with fresh cilantro.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Chill your cucumber slices in ice water before mixing - this keeps them extra crisp and refreshing
  • Don't overdress the salad; the ponzu should coat lightly without pooling at the bottom
  • Serve this salad within 30 minutes of preparation to maintain the best texture and prevent the vegetables from wilting
