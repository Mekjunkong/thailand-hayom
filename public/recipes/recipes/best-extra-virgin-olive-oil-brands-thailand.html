👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Start by understanding that Extra Virgin Olive Oil (EVOO) is cold-pressed from fresh olives without heat or chemicals, preserving maximum nutrients and flavor.
  2. Look for dark glass bottles or tins that protect the oil from light oxidation, which can destroy beneficial compounds and create off-flavors.
  3. Check the harvest date rather than expiration date - fresher oils (within 18 months of harvest) retain more polyphenols and antioxidants.
  4. Consider your intended use: robust oils like Picual variety work well for cooking, while mild Arbequina varieties are perfect for drizzling and salads.
  5. Store your chosen oil in a cool, dark place away from heat sources like stoves or direct sunlight to maintain quality.
  6. Taste-test different brands to find your preference - some prefer peppery, bitter notes while others enjoy smooth, fruity profiles.
  7. Buy appropriate quantities based on usage - even the best oil degrades over time once opened, so smaller bottles may be better for occasional users.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Look for oils with high polyphenol content (above 250mg/kg) for maximum health benefits and longer shelf life
  • Spanish Picual variety offers the most robust flavor and highest antioxidant content, perfect for those new to premium olive oils
  • Never use extra virgin olive oil for high-heat cooking above 375°F - save it for low-heat sautéing, salad dressings, and finishing dishes
