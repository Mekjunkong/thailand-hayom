Tom Jap Chai — Clear Thai Vegetable Soup with Tofu and Mushrooms

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

Tom Jap Chai is the ultimate Thai comfort soup — a clear, warming broth filled with tender vegetables and silky tofu that's both nourishing and satisfying. This Thai-Chinese fusion dish proves that the simplest ingredients can create the most comforting meals, making it a perfect weeknight dinner when you crave something light yet fulfilling.

💡 Tom Jap Chai is a beloved Thai-Chinese comfort soup that translates to 'mixed vegetable soup.' This humble dish reflects the Chinese influence in Thai cuisine and is often enjoyed as a light, nourishing meal that's easy on the stomach.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1/4 head napa cabbage, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 medium daikon radish, sliced into rounds
  • 1 block (7 oz) firm white tofu, cut into cubes
  • 5-6 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and sliced
  • 4-5 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper powder

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in warm water for 15 minutes until softened, then drain and slice them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes and slice the daikon radish into 1/4-inch rounds. Chop the napa cabbage into 2-inch pieces.
  3. In a large pot, bring the chicken or vegetable stock to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the daikon radish first and cook for 5 minutes, as it takes longer to become tender.
  5. Add the shiitake mushrooms and tofu cubes to the pot. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add the napa cabbage pieces and cook for another 2-3 minutes until just wilted but still crisp.
  7. Season with light soy sauce, sugar, and white pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  8. Serve immediately while hot, ladled into individual bowls.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the vegetables — they should retain some crispness for the best texture contrast
  • Save the mushroom soaking liquid and add it to your stock for extra umami depth
  • This soup tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together beautifully
