Thai Chicken Satay (Gai Satay) — Grilled Street Food Perfection

⏱ 60 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

Nothing captures the essence of Thai street food quite like perfectly grilled chicken satay with its aromatic turmeric-coconut marinade. These golden skewers are a crowd-pleaser that brings the bustling energy of Bangkok's street markets right to your backyard grill.

thai foodgrilled chickenstreet foodparty appetizersatay
💡 Satay originated from Indonesia but has become deeply embedded in Thai street food culture. Thai vendors often serve it with a sweet-tangy peanut sauce and fresh cucumber relish, making it a perfect balance of flavors that represents the essence of Thai cuisine.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced into long strips
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seed powder
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Ready-made peanut sauce or homemade
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine turmeric powder, coriander powder, palm sugar, salt, vegetable oil, coconut milk, and fish sauce to create the marinade.
  2. Add chicken strips to the marinade and mix well to coat evenly. Marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for best flavor.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken strips onto soaked bamboo skewers, making sure pieces are evenly distributed.
  4. Preheat grill or grill pan to medium heat. Grill the chicken skewers for 3-4 minutes per side, brushing with a little oil while cooking.
  5. Continue grilling until chicken is fully cooked and golden brown with nice char marks, about 10-12 minutes total.
  6. Serve hot with peanut sauce, cucumber salad, and sticky rice for an authentic Thai street food experience.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Marinate overnight for maximum flavor penetration - the coconut milk tenderizes the meat beautifully
  • Don't skip soaking the bamboo skewers to prevent burning during grilling
  • Brush with oil during grilling to keep the chicken moist and achieve that perfect golden color
