Thai Seafood Tom Yum Fried Rice — Creamy & Spicy One-Pan Wonder

⏱ 25 min 🍽 Serves 2-3 📊 Easy

This creamy tom yum fried rice brings together two of Thailand's most beloved flavors in one satisfying dish. The roasted chili paste adds depth and smokiness while fresh seafood and a splash of cream create a restaurant-quality meal that's ready in just 25 minutes.

thai fried riceseafoodtom yumspicyone pan meal
💡 This fusion dish combines the beloved flavors of tom yum soup with Thailand's most popular comfort food — fried rice. The addition of cream creates a rich, restaurant-style texture that's become popular in modern Thai cooking.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 3.5 oz fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3.5 oz squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 tablespoon roasted chili paste (nam prik pao)
  • 3 tablespoons whole milk or heavy cream
  • 2-3 cups day-old cooked jasmine rice
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • Fresh lime juice to taste
  • Sugar to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the roasted chili paste and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add the shrimp and squid to the wok. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the seafood is nearly cooked through and changes color.
  3. Push seafood to one side of the wok and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until just set, then mix with the seafood.
  4. Add the day-old rice, breaking up any clumps with your spatula. Stir-fry everything together for 2-3 minutes until the rice is heated through and well combined.
  5. Pour in the milk or cream and toss everything together. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and a pinch of sugar to balance the flavors.
  6. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped green onions and fresh cilantro. Serve immediately while hot.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use day-old rice that's been refrigerated — fresh rice will become mushy and won't achieve the proper texture
  • Don't overcook the seafood as it will become rubbery; add it just long enough to cook through
  • Adjust the chili paste, fish sauce, and lime juice to your taste — the dish should be spicy, salty, and slightly tangy
