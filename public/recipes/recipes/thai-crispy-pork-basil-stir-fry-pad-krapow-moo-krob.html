Thai Crispy Pork Basil Stir-Fry (Pad Krapow Moo Krob) — Restaurant-Style in 10 Minutes

⏱ 10 min 🍽 Serves 2 📊 Easy

When Thais need comfort food fast, they turn to pad krapow — and this crispy pork version takes it to the next level. With crackling pork belly, fiery chilies, and aromatic holy basil, this is the kind of dish that makes you understand why Thai street food is legendary.

💡 Pad Krapow is Thailand's ultimate comfort food and a lunchtime staple found at every street food stall. The key is using holy basil (bai krapow), not Thai sweet basil, which gives this dish its distinctive peppery, almost clove-like flavor that makes it so addictive.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 7 oz crispy pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 5-8 Thai bird's eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 5-6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 large handful fresh holy basil leaves (about 1 cup packed)
  • 1-2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil for stir-frying

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic together until you have a rough paste. If you don't have a mortar, finely mince both ingredients together.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking hot. Add the chili-garlic paste and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the crispy pork pieces and toss quickly to coat with the aromatics. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until heated through.
  4. Add oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and fish sauce. Toss everything together for another minute, adjusting seasonings to taste.
  5. Turn off the heat and immediately add the holy basil leaves. Toss quickly so the basil wilts from the residual heat but retains its fresh aroma.
  6. Serve immediately over steamed jasmine rice, preferably topped with a fried egg for the complete experience.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use the highest heat possible and work quickly — the entire stir-fry should take no more than 3-4 minutes once you start cooking
  • Don't skip the mortar and pestle for the chili-garlic paste; it releases oils that mincing can't achieve and makes all the difference in flavor
  • Add the holy basil at the very end with the heat off to preserve its aromatic oils — overcooked basil becomes bitter
