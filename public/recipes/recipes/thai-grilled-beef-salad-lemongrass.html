Thai Grilled Beef Salad with Lemongrass — Yam Neua Yang Takrai

⏱ 30 min 🍽 Serves 4 📊 Medium

This vibrant Thai grilled beef salad brings together the aromatic essence of lemongrass with perfectly seasoned beef in a zesty lime dressing. It's the kind of dish that wakes up your taste buds with its bold combination of fresh herbs, spicy chilies, and that distinctive Thai balance of flavors that keeps you coming back for more.

💡 This refreshing Thai salad showcases the perfect balance of hot, sour, salty, and aromatic flavors that define authentic Thai cuisine. The combination of grilled beef with fragrant lemongrass and herbs makes it a popular dish in northeastern Thailand, often enjoyed with sticky rice and cold beer.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or top round, sliced thin
  • 3 stalks lemongrass, tender parts only, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 6-8 kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • 3-4 Thai bird's eye chilies, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • Lettuce leaves for serving

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Grill or pan-sear the beef slices over high heat until just cooked through but still tender, about 2-3 minutes per side. Let cool slightly and slice into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic together until you have a rough paste.
  3. Add fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar to the chili-garlic mixture, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the grilled beef, sliced lemongrass, red onion, and shredded kaffir lime leaves.
  5. Pour the dressing over the beef mixture and toss everything together until well coated.
  6. Add fresh mint leaves and give one final gentle toss. Serve immediately with lettuce leaves on the side.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the beef — it should remain tender and juicy for the best texture contrast with the crisp herbs
  • Slice the lemongrass paper-thin using only the tender white and light green parts to avoid any tough, fibrous pieces
  • Serve immediately after mixing to maintain the fresh, vibrant flavors and prevent the herbs from wilting
