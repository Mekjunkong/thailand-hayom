Fragrant Durian Coconut Bread — Soft Vegan Thai Durian Loaf

⏱ 185 min 🍽 Makes 1 loaf (8-10 slices) 📊 Medium

For durian enthusiasts, this fragrant Thai durian bread is pure heaven in loaf form. The combination of rich coconut milk and sweet, creamy durian creates an incredibly soft and aromatic bread that's surprisingly vegan-friendly.

💡 Durian bread is a beloved Thai bakery treat that celebrates our 'king of fruits.' This vegan version maintains all the aromatic intensity and creamy sweetness that makes durian so cherished in Thai cuisine, perfect for adventurous palates.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 1 cup pureed durian flesh (about 8-10 oz)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for durian mixture)
  • Sliced almonds for topping (optional)

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together bread flour, yeast, salt, and 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix coconut milk with cold water until smooth.
  3. Create a well in the flour mixture and pour in the coconut milk mixture. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  5. Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Meanwhile, mix the pureed durian flesh with 2 tablespoons sugar until well combined. Set aside.
  7. Once risen, punch down the dough and roll it into a rectangle about 12x8 inches.
  8. Spread the sweetened durian mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border on all sides.
  9. Roll the dough tightly from the long side, pinch the seam to seal, and place seam-side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  10. Cover and let rise for another 45 minutes until the dough crowns above the pan rim.
  11. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the top with coconut milk and sprinkle with sliced almonds if using.
  12. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 190°F (88°C).
  13. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Use ripe, fragrant durian for the best flavor — the flesh should be creamy and easily mashable with a fork
  • Don't overbake as the durian can become bitter; the bread is done when it sounds hollow when tapped
  • Let the bread cool completely before slicing to prevent the durian filling from oozing out
