Best Extra Virgin Olive Oil Brands for Thai Cooking — Premium Cold-Pressed Guide

⏱ 5 min 🍽 Guide for selecting olive oil 📊 Easy

Choosing the right extra virgin olive oil can elevate your Thai cooking from good to extraordinary. While traditional Thai cuisine relies on coconut and peanut oils, modern Thai kitchens are embracing high-quality olive oils for their health benefits and unique flavor profiles that complement both traditional and fusion dishes.

💡 While not traditional in Thai cooking, high-quality extra virgin olive oil has found its way into modern Thai kitchens, especially for health-conscious cooks and fusion dishes. Many Thai chefs now use it to finish salads or create unique dressing blends that complement traditional flavors.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Extra virgin olive oil (any brand from recommendations)
  • Your favorite Thai ingredients for drizzling or finishing dishes

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Choose your preferred extra virgin olive oil based on flavor profile - mild brands like Ybarra for delicate dishes, or robust options like Rawganiq for bold flavors.
  2. Store olive oil in a cool, dark place away from heat and light to preserve quality and prevent oxidation.
  3. Use extra virgin olive oil for finishing Thai salads, drizzling over grilled meats, or making herb-infused oils for som tam dressing.
  4. For cooking at higher temperatures, reserve extra virgin olive oil for final touches and use regular olive oil for actual cooking.
  5. Taste different brands to find your preference - some are fruity and mild, others are peppery and intense.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Look for harvest dates rather than expiration dates - fresher oil within 18 months of harvest tastes significantly better
  • Dark glass bottles protect the oil from light damage better than clear containers - this preserves the delicate flavors longer
  • Start with a mid-range brand like Filippo Berio before investing in premium organic options to develop your palate
