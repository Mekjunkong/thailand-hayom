The Complete Guide to Extra Virgin Olive Oil — Best Brands for Thai Cooking

⏱ 0 min 🍽 Guide for selecting olive oil 📊 Easy

Choosing the right extra virgin olive oil can elevate your Thai cooking, especially for fresh salads and finishing touches. While traditional Thai cuisine relies on coconut and peanut oils, modern health-conscious cooks are discovering how premium EVOO enhances dishes like som tam and larb without overwhelming their delicate flavors.

💡 While olive oil isn't traditional in Thai cuisine, modern Thai cooks increasingly use extra virgin olive oil for its health benefits in salads like som tam and yam wun sen. The key is choosing oils that complement rather than overpower the delicate balance of Thai flavors.

🛒 Ingredients

  • Extra virgin olive oil - various brands (Filippo Berio, Fragata, Agrilife, Rawganiq, Clemente, Ybarra, Romulo, Naturel)
  • Quality assessment criteria: cold-pressed, organic certification, proper packaging

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Look for cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil with organic certification when possible. Check that the bottle is dark glass to protect from light oxidation.
  2. For Thai cooking applications, choose oils with moderate acidity levels and fresh harvest dates. Spanish Picual varieties offer robust flavor perfect for drizzling over som tam or larb.
  3. Store your selected olive oil in a cool, dark place away from heat sources. Use within 18-24 months of harvest date for optimal flavor and nutritional benefits.
  4. Taste test different brands to find your preference - some prefer the peppery bite of Agrilife Organic, while others enjoy the milder Arbequina variety from Romulo.
  5. Use higher quality oils for finishing dishes and salads, while reserving standard grades for light cooking at low temperatures.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Choose Spanish Picual varieties for their high polyphenol content and robust flavor that pairs well with spicy Thai salads
  • Always check the harvest date rather than expiration date - fresher oils within 12 months of harvest offer the best flavor and nutrition
  • Store opened bottles in the refrigerator if you live in a hot climate to prevent rancidity and maintain quality longer
