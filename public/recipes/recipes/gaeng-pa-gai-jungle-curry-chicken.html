Gaeng Pa Gai — Spicy Thai Jungle Curry with Chicken

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 3-4 📊 Medium

Get ready for some serious heat with Gaeng Pa Gai, Thailand's legendary jungle curry that pulls no punches. Unlike other Thai curries, this fiery forest favorite skips the coconut milk entirely, letting the pure intensity of chilies and aromatic herbs take center stage. It's the kind of curry that'll make you sweat, smile, and reach for more rice all at the same time.

thai curryspicy foodjungle currychicken curryauthentic thai
💡 Gaeng Pa, or jungle curry, originates from Thailand's forest regions where coconut milk wasn't readily available. This intensely spicy curry relies purely on herbs, chilies, and the natural flavors of the ingredients, making it one of the hottest curries in Thai cuisine.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 10.5 oz chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3-4 tablespoons jungle curry paste (gaeng pa paste)
  • 3-4 pieces fresh galangal root, sliced
  • 1 small bunch fresh green peppercorns
  • Thai basil leaves
  • Kaffir lime leaves
  • Fish sauce, to taste
  • Palm sugar or brown sugar, to taste
  • 2 cups water or chicken stock

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Heat a wok or large pan over medium-high heat without oil. Add the jungle curry paste and dry-fry for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and the oils start to separate.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and stir-fry with the curry paste for 3-4 minutes until the chicken is sealed and coated with the paste.
  3. Pour in the water or chicken stock gradually, stirring to dissolve the curry paste completely. Bring to a boil.
  4. Add the sliced galangal and fresh green peppercorns. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 10-12 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar to taste, balancing salty, spicy, and slightly sweet flavors.
  6. Add torn kaffir lime leaves and Thai basil leaves in the last minute of cooking. Stir gently and remove from heat.
  7. Serve immediately with steamed jasmine rice.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Dry-frying the curry paste first is crucial - it develops deeper flavors and prevents the curry from tasting raw
  • Don't add coconut milk to jungle curry - it's meant to be a clear, brothy curry that showcases the pure heat of chilies
  • Fresh green peppercorns add a unique numbing spiciness - if you can't find them, substitute with a few extra Thai chilies
