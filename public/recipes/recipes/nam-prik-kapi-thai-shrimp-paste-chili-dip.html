Nam Prik Kapi — Authentic Thai Shrimp Paste Chili Dip

⏱ 15 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 as a condiment 📊 Easy

Nam Prik Kapi is the soul of Thai cuisine - a fiery, funky chili dip that transforms simple vegetables into an addictive feast. This ancient condiment combines the deep umami of fermented shrimp paste with blazing chilies and aromatic garlic, creating a flavor bomb that's both primal and sophisticated.

thai condimentshrimp pastespicy dipauthentic thainam prik
💡 Nam Prik Kapi is one of Thailand's most fundamental condiments, found on tables across the country. This intensely flavored dip represents the perfect balance of Thai flavors and is traditionally eaten with fresh vegetables like cucumber, green beans, and cabbage.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (kapi)
  • 5-8 Thai bird's eye chilies (prik kee noo)
  • 3-4 garlic cloves
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Fish sauce, to taste
  • Palm sugar or brown sugar, to taste

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the chilies and garlic together until roughly crushed. Don't make it completely smooth - you want some texture.
  2. Heat a dry pan or wok over medium heat and add the shrimp paste. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant and slightly darkened.
  3. Add the pounded chili-garlic mixture to the pan with the shrimp paste. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until well combined and aromatic.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Stir in fresh lime juice, then season with fish sauce and sugar to balance the flavors.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning - it should be salty, spicy, and slightly sweet with a bright lime finish.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Toast the shrimp paste until it's fragrant and loses its raw smell - this step is crucial for developing deep umami flavor
  • Use a traditional granite mortar and pestle if possible, as it creates the perfect texture that releases maximum flavor from the chilies
  • Serve immediately with fresh raw vegetables, steamed rice, or use as a flavor base for other Thai dishes
