Thai Milk-Marinated Grilled Pork — Tender Morning Street Food Delight

⏱ 60 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

Nothing beats the aroma of Thai grilled pork sizzling over charcoal in the early morning hours. This milk-marinated version creates incredibly tender, slightly sweet pork that melts in your mouth — a beloved street food that brings the authentic flavors of Thailand right to your backyard grill.

thai foodgrilled porkstreet foodbreakfastmarinated meat
💡 This traditional Thai breakfast dish showcases the ingenious use of fresh milk as a tenderizer, a technique passed down through generations of street food vendors. The milk proteins break down the meat fibers while adding a subtle sweetness that perfectly complements the smoky char from grilling.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1.1 lbs pork shoulder or tenderloin, sliced thin
  • 3 tablespoons whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • Small amount of vegetable oil for brushing

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Combine pork slices with milk, palm sugar, soy sauce, and minced garlic in a bowl. Mix thoroughly to ensure all pieces are well coated.
  2. Marinate the pork for at least 30 minutes, though overnight marination will yield even more tender and flavorful results.
  3. Thread the marinated pork onto flat bamboo skewers, making sure pieces lay flat for even cooking.
  4. Grill over medium heat, turning frequently and brushing lightly with oil to prevent sticking. Cook until the pork is golden brown and cooked through, about 12-15 minutes total.
  5. Serve hot with sticky rice and your favorite Thai dipping sauce.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Slice the pork against the grain and no thicker than 1/4 inch for maximum tenderness
  • Soak bamboo skewers in water for 30 minutes before threading to prevent burning
  • Keep the grill at medium heat and move skewers frequently to avoid charring while ensuring even cooking
