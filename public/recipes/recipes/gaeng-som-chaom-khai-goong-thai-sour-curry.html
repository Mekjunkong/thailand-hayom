Gaeng Som Chaom Khai Goong — Thai Sour Curry with Acacia Leaves and Shrimp

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 3-4 📊 Easy

When the monsoon rains arrive in Thailand, home cooks reach for gaeng som - a vibrant, tangy curry that's like sunshine in a bowl. This traditional sour curry combines the unique flavor of acacia leaves with tender shrimp and silky egg ribbons in a broth that's both comforting and invigorating.

💡 Gaeng som is a beloved rainy season dish in Thailand, known for its refreshing sour and spicy flavors that cut through humid weather. Acacia leaves (chaom) add a unique slightly bitter note that balances the curry's tanginess perfectly.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh acacia leaves (chaom), about 4 oz
  • 2 large eggs
  • 5 oz fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons sour curry paste (nam prik gaeng som)
  • 2 tablespoons wet tamarind paste
  • Palm sugar to taste
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • 4 cups water

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a medium saucepan.
  2. Add the sour curry paste to the boiling water and stir until completely dissolved. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the wet tamarind paste and stir well. The broth should have a beautiful orange-red color.
  4. Crack the eggs into a small bowl and lightly beat them. Slowly drizzle the beaten eggs into the simmering curry while stirring gently to create silky egg ribbons.
  5. Add the shrimp to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and are cooked through.
  6. Add the acacia leaves and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the leaves are wilted but still bright green.
  7. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar to taste. The flavor should be predominantly sour and spicy, with a hint of sweetness.
  8. Remove from heat and serve immediately with steamed jasmine rice.

💡 Pro Tips

  • If you can't find fresh acacia leaves, substitute with young tamarind leaves or even spinach, though the flavor will be different
  • The key to silky egg ribbons is to drizzle the beaten eggs very slowly while stirring the simmering curry continuously
  • Taste and adjust the sourness with more tamarind paste - the curry should make your mouth pucker slightly
