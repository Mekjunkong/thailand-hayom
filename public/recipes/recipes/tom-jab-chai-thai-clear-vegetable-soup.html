Tom Jab Chai — Thai Clear Vegetable Soup with Tofu

⏱ 35 min 🍽 Serves 4-6 📊 Easy

Tom Jab Chai is Thai comfort food at its finest — a crystal-clear soup that lets each ingredient shine with pure, clean flavors. This gentle, nourishing soup combines tender vegetables and silky tofu in a savory broth that's both satisfying and light. It's the perfect antidote to spicy Thai meals and a wonderful way to showcase the Thai philosophy that simple ingredients, treated with care, can create something truly special.

💡 Tom Jab Chai is a beloved Thai comfort soup that showcases the beauty of simplicity in Thai cuisine. This clear, light soup is often served as part of a larger meal to balance richer, spicier dishes, and is particularly popular during cooler weather or when someone needs gentle, nourishing food.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1/4 medium head cabbage, chopped into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 medium daikon radish, sliced into rounds
  • 1 block (7 oz) firm white tofu, cut into cubes
  • 5-6 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and sliced
  • 4-5 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper, or to taste

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in warm water for 15 minutes until softened, then drain and slice them.
  2. Bring the chicken or vegetable stock to a gentle boil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the daikon radish slices first and simmer for 5 minutes until they start to soften.
  4. Add the shiitake mushrooms and tofu cubes, then simmer for another 3-4 minutes.
  5. Add the cabbage pieces and cook for 2-3 minutes until just wilted but still crisp.
  6. Season with light soy sauce, sugar, and white pepper to taste. The soup should have a clean, savory flavor.
  7. Serve immediately while hot, ladled into individual bowls.

💡 Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook the vegetables — they should retain some crunch for the best texture contrast
  • Save the mushroom soaking liquid and add it to your stock for extra umami depth
  • Taste and adjust the seasoning at the end — the balance of salty, sweet, and peppery flavors is key to this soup's charm
